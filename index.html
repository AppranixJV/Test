Historical Events for 01/14

  • Year: 2019 - A Saha Airlines Boeing 707 crashes at Fath Air Base near Karaj in Alborz Province, Iran, killing 15 people.
  • Year: 2011 - President Zine El Abidine Ben Ali of Tunisia seeks refuge in Saudi Arabia after a series of demonstrations against his regime, considered to be the birth of the Arab Spring.
  • Year: 2010 - Yemen declares an open war against the terrorist group al-Qaeda.
  • Year: 2004 - The national flag of the Republic of Georgia, the so-called "five cross flag", is restored to official use after a hiatus of some 500 years.
  • Year: 1993 - In Poland's worst peacetime maritime disaster, ferry MS Jan Heweliusz sinks off the coast of Rügen, drowning 55 passengers and crew; nine crew-members are saved.
  • Year: 1973 - Elvis Presley's concert Aloha from Hawaii is broadcast live via satellite, and sets the record as the most watched broadcast by an individual entertainer in television history.
  • Year: 1972 - Queen Margrethe II of Denmark ascends the throne, the first Queen of Denmark since 1412 and the first Danish monarch not named Frederick or Christian since 1513.
  • Year: 1969 - USS Enterprise fire: An accidental explosion aboard the USS Enterprise near Hawaii kills 28 people.
  • Year: 1967 - Counterculture of the 1960s: The Human Be-In takes place in San Francisco, California's Golden Gate Park, launching the Summer of Love.
  • Year: 1960 - The Reserve Bank of Australia, the country's central bank and banknote issuing authority authorized by the 1959 Reserve Bank Act, is established.
  • Year: 1957 - Kripalu Maharaj is named fifth Jagadguru (world teacher) after giving seven days of speeches before 500 Hindu scholars.
  • Year: 1954 - The Hudson Motor Car Company merges with Nash-Kelvinator Corporation forming the American Motors Corporation.
  • Year: 1953 - Josip Broz Tito is elected the first President of Yugoslavia.
  • Year: 1952 - NBC's long-running morning news program Today debuts, with host Dave Garroway.
  • Year: 1951 - National Airlines Flight 83 crashes during landing at Philadelphia International Airport, killing seven passengers and crew.
  • Year: 1943 - World War II: Franklin D. Roosevelt and Winston Churchill begin the Casablanca Conference to discuss strategy and study the next phase of the war.
  • Year: 1943 - World War II: Japan begins Operation Ke, the successful operation to evacuate its forces from Guadalcanal during the Guadalcanal Campaign.
  • Year: 1939 - Norway claims Queen Maud Land in Antarctica.
  • Year: 1911 - Roald Amundsen's South Pole expedition makes landfall on the eastern edge of the Ross Ice Shelf.
  • Year: 1907 - An earthquake in Kingston, Jamaica kills more than 1,000 people.
  • Year: 1900 - Giacomo Puccini's Tosca opens in Rome.
  • Year: 1899 - RMS Oceanic (1899) is launched. She is the largest ship afloat since Brunel's SS Great Eastern.
  • Year: 1858 - Napoleon III of France escapes an assassination attempt made by Felice Orsini and his accomplices in Paris.
  • Year: 1814 - Treaty of Kiel: Frederick VI of Denmark cedes the Kingdom of Norway to Charles XIII of Sweden in return for Pomerania.
  • Year: 1784 - American Revolutionary War: Ratification Day, United States - Congress ratifies the Treaty of Paris with Great Britain.
  • Year: 1761 - The Third Battle of Panipat is fought in India between the Afghans under Ahmad Shah Durrani and the Marathas.
  • Year: 1639 - The "Fundamental Orders", the first written constitution that created a government, is adopted in Connecticut.
  • Year: 1301 - Andrew III of Hungary dies, ending the Árpád dynasty in Hungary.
  • Year: 1236 - King Henry III of England marries Eleanor of Provence.
