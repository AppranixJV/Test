Historical Events for 10/09

  • Year: 2019 - Turkey begins its military offensive in north-eastern Syria.
  • Year: 2016 - The Arakan Rohingya Salvation Army launches its first attack on Myanmar security forces along the Bangladesh-Myanmar border.
  • Year: 2012 - Pakistani Taliban attempt to assassinate outspoken schoolgirl Malala Yousafzai.
  • Year: 2009 - First lunar impact of NASA's Lunar Precursor Robotic Program.
  • Year: 2007 - The Dow Jones Industrial Average reaches its all-time high of 14,164 points before rapidly declining due to the 2007-2008 financial crises.
  • Year: 2006 - North Korea conducts its first nuclear test.
  • Year: 1995 - An Amtrak Sunset Limited train is derailed by saboteurs near Palo Verde, Arizona.
  • Year: 1992 - The Peekskill meteorite, a 27.7 pounds (12.6 kg) meteorite crashed into a parked car in Peekskill, New York
  • Year: 1986 - Fox Broadcasting Company (FBC) launches as the "fourth" US television network.
  • Year: 1986 - The Phantom of the Opera, eventually the second longest running musical in London, opens at Her Majesty's Theatre.
  • Year: 1984 - The popular children's television show Thomas The Tank Engine & Friends, based on The Railway Series by the Reverend Wilbert Awdry, premieres on ITV.
  • Year: 1983 - South Korean President Chun Doo-hwan survives an assassination attempt in Rangoon, Burma (present-day Yangon, Myanmar), but the blast kills 21 and injures 17 others.
  • Year: 1981 - President François Mitterrand abolishes capital punishment in France.
  • Year: 1980 - Pope John Paul II greets the Dalai Lama during a private audience in Vatican City.
  • Year: 1970 - The Khmer Republic is proclaimed in Cambodia.
  • Year: 1969 - In Chicago, the National Guard is called in as demonstrations continue over the trial of the "Chicago Eight".
  • Year: 1967 - A day after his capture, Ernesto "Che" Guevara is executed for attempting to incite a revolution in Bolivia.
  • Year: 1966 - Vietnam War: the Republic of Korea Army commits the Binh Tai Massacre.
  • Year: 1963 - In Italy, a large landslide causes a giant wave to overtop the Vajont Dam, killing over 2,000.
  • Year: 1962 - Uganda becomes an independent Commonwealth realm.
  • Year: 1950 - The Goyang Geumjeong Cave massacre in Korea begins.
  • Year: 1942 - Australia's Statute of Westminster Adoption Act 1942 receives royal assent.
  • Year: 1941 - A coup in Panama declares Ricardo Adolfo de la Guardia Arango the new president.
  • Year: 1937 - Murder of 9 Catholic priests in Zhengding, China, who protected the local population from the advancing Japanese army.
  • Year: 1936 - Boulder Dam (later Hoover Dam) begins to generate electricity and transmit it to Los Angeles.
  • Year: 1934 - An Ustashe assassin kills King Alexander I of Yugoslavia and Louis Barthou, Foreign Minister of France, in Marseille.
  • Year: 1919 - The Cincinnati Reds win the World Series, resulting in the Black Sox Scandal.
  • Year: 1918 - The Finnish Parliament offers to Prince Frederick Charles of Hesse the throne of a short-lived Kingdom of Finland.
  • Year: 1914 - World War I: The Siege of Antwerp comes to an end.
  • Year: 1913 - The steamship SS Volturno catches fire in the mid-Atlantic.
  • Year: 1911 - An accidental bomb explosion triggers the Wuchang Uprising against the Qing dynasty, beginning the Xinhai Revolution.
  • Year: 1900 - The Cook Islands become a territory of the United Kingdom.
  • Year: 1874 - The Universal Postal Union is created by the Treaty of Bern.
  • Year: 1873 - A meeting at the U.S. Naval Academy establishes the U.S. Naval Institute.
  • Year: 1864 - American Civil War: Union cavalrymen defeat Confederate forces at Toms Brook, Virginia.
  • Year: 1861 - American Civil War: Union troops repel a Confederate attempt to capture Fort Pickens at the Battle of Santa Rosa Island.
  • Year: 1854 - Crimean War: The siege of Sevastopol begins.
  • Year: 1847 - Slavery is abolished in the Swedish colony of Saint Barthélemy.
  • Year: 1834 - Opening of the Dublin and Kingstown Railway, the first public railway on the island of Ireland.
  • Year: 1831 - Ioannis Kapodistrias, the first head of state of the Kingdom of Greece, is assassinated.
  • Year: 1825 - Restauration arrives in New York Harbor from Norway, the first organized immigration from Norway to the United States.
  • Year: 1820 - Guayaquil declares independence from Spain.
  • Year: 1812 - War of 1812: In a naval engagement on Lake Erie, American forces capture two British ships: HMS Detroit and HMS Caledonia.
  • Year: 1806 - Prussia begins the War of the Fourth Coalition against France.
  • Year: 1804 - Hobart, capital of Tasmania, is founded.
  • Year: 1799 - HMS Lutine sinks with the loss of 240 men and a cargo worth £1,200,000.
  • Year: 1790 - A severe earthquake in northern Algeria causes severe damage and a tsunami in the Mediterranean Sea and kills three thousand.
  • Year: 1760 - Seven Years' War: Russian and Austrian troops briefly occupy Berlin.
  • Year: 1740 - Dutch colonists and Javanese natives begin a massacre of the ethnic Chinese population in Batavia, eventually killing at least 10,000.
  • Year: 1708 - Peter the Great defeats the Swedes at the Battle of Lesnaya.
  • Year: 1701 - The Collegiate School of Connecticut (later renamed Yale University) is chartered in Old Saybrook.
  • Year: 1635 - Roger Williams is banished from the Massachusetts Bay Colony after religious and policy disagreements.
  • Year: 1604 - Kepler's Supernova is the most recent supernova to be observed within the Milky Way.
  • Year: 1594 - Troops of the Portuguese Empire are defeated on Sri Lanka, bringing an end to the Campaign of Danture.
  • Year: 1446 - The Hangul alphabet is published in Korea.
  • Year: 1410 - The first known mention of the Prague astronomical clock.
  • Year: 1238 - James I of Aragon founds the Kingdom of Valencia.
  • Year: 768 - Carloman I and Charlemagne are crowned kings of the Franks.
