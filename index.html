Historical Events for 01/02

  • Year: 2022 - Massive nationwide protests and unrest break out in Kazakhstan over the sudden increase of liquefied petroleum gas, leaving over 200 people dead and thousands injured.
  • Year: 2004 - Stardust successfully flies past Comet Wild 2, collecting samples that are returned to Earth.
  • Year: 1993 - Sri Lankan Civil War: The Sri Lanka Navy kill 35-100 civilians on the Jaffna Lagoon.
  • Year: 1991 - Sharon Pratt Dixon becomes the first African American woman mayor of a major city and first woman Mayor of the District of Columbia.
  • Year: 1988 - Condor Flugdienst Flight 3782 crashes near Seferihisar, Turkey, killing 16 people.
  • Year: 1981 - One of the largest investigations by a British police force ends when serial killer Peter Sutcliffe, the "Yorkshire Ripper", is arrested in Sheffield, South Yorkshire.
  • Year: 1978 - On the orders of the President of Pakistan, Muhammad Zia-ul-Haq, paramilitary forces opened fire on peaceful protesting workers in Multan, Pakistan; it is known as 1978 massacre at Multan Colony Textile Mills.
  • Year: 1976 - The Gale of January 1976 begins, resulting in coastal flooding around the southern North Sea coasts, affecting countries from Ireland to Yugoslavia and causing at least 82 deaths and US$1.3 billion in damage.
  • Year: 1975 - The Federal Rules of Evidence are approved by the United States Congress.
  • Year: 1975 - At the opening of a new railway line, a bomb blast at Samastipur, Bihar, India, fatally wounds Lalit Narayan Mishra, Minister of Railways.
  • Year: 1974 - United States President Richard Nixon signs a bill lowering the maximum U.S. speed limit to 55 mph in order to conserve gasoline during an OPEC embargo.
  • Year: 1971 - The second Ibrox disaster kills 66 fans at a Rangers-Celtic association football (soccer) match.
  • Year: 1967 - Ronald Reagan, past movie actor and future President of the United States, is sworn in as Governor of California.
  • Year: 1963 - Vietnam War: The Viet Cong wins its first major victory, at the Battle of Ap Bac.
  • Year: 1959 - Luna 1, the first spacecraft to reach the vicinity of the Moon and to orbit the Sun, is launched by the Soviet Union.
  • Year: 1955 - Following the assassination of the Panamanian president José Antonio Remón Cantera, his deputy, José Ramón Guizado, takes power, but is quickly deposed after his involvement in Cantera's death is discovered.
  • Year: 1954 - India establishes its highest civilian awards, the Bharat Ratna and the Padma Vibhushan.
  • Year: 1949 - Luis Muñoz Marín is inaugurated as the first democratically elected Governor of Puerto Rico.
  • Year: 1942 - World War II: Manila is captured by Japanese forces, enabling them to control the Philippines.
  • Year: 1942 - The Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) obtains the conviction of 33 members of a German spy ring headed by Fritz Joubert Duquesne in the largest espionage case in United States history; Also known as the Duquesne Spy Ring.
  • Year: 1941 - World War II: The Cardiff Blitz severely damages the cathedral in Cardiff, Wales.
  • Year: 1921 - World premiere of the science fiction play R.U.R. by the Czech writer Karel Čapek in a theater in Hradec Králové.
  • Year: 1920 - The second Palmer Raid, ordered by the US Department of Justice, results in 6,000 suspected communists and anarchists being arrested and held without trial.
  • Year: 1900 - Chicago Canal opens.
  • Year: 1900 - American statesman and diplomat John Hay announces the Open Door Policy to promote trade with China.
  • Year: 1865 - Uruguayan War: The Siege of Paysandú ends as the Brazilians and Coloradans capture Paysandú, Uruguay.
  • Year: 1818 - The British Institution of Civil Engineers is founded by a group of six engineers; Thomas Telford would later become its first president.
  • Year: 1791 - Big Bottom massacre in the Ohio Country, North America, marking the beginning of the Northwest Indian War.
  • Year: 1788 - Georgia becomes the fourth state to ratify the United States Constitution.
  • Year: 1777 - American Revolutionary War: American forces under the command of George Washington repulsed a British attack at the Battle of the Assunpink Creek near Trenton, New Jersey.
  • Year: 1680 - Trunajaya rebellion: Amangkurat II of Mataram and his bodyguards execute the rebel leader Trunajaya.
  • Year: 1492 - Reconquista: The Emirate of Granada, the last Moorish stronghold in Spain, surrenders.
  • Year: 533 - Mercurius becomes Pope John II, the first pope to adopt a new name upon elevation to the papacy.
  • Year: 366 - The Alemanni cross the frozen Rhine in large numbers, invading the Roman Empire.
  • Year: 69 - The Roman legions in Germania Superior refuse to swear loyalty to Galba. They rebel and proclaim Vitellius as emperor.
