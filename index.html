Historical Events for 02/08

  • Year: 2014 - A hotel fire in Medina, Saudi Arabia, kills 15 Egyptian pilgrims with 130 others injured.
  • Year: 2013 - A blizzard kills at least 18 and leaves hundreds of thousands of people without electricity in the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.
  • Year: 2010 - Over 2 miles (3.2 km) of road are buried after a storm in the Hindu Kush mountains of Afghanistan triggers a series of avalanches, killing at least 172 people and trapping over 2,000 others.
  • Year: 1993 - An Iran Air Tours Tupolev Tu-154 and an Iranian Air Force Sukhoi Su-24 collide mid-air near Qods, Iran, killing all 133 people onboard both aircraft.
  • Year: 1989 - Independent Air Flight 1851 strikes Pico Alto mountain while on approach to Santa Maria Airport, killing all 144 passengers on board.
  • Year: 1986 - Twenty-three people are killed when a VIA Rail passenger train collides with a Canadian National freight train near the town of Hinton, Alberta, making it one of the worst rail accidents in Canada.
  • Year: 1983 - Irish race horse Shergar is stolen and allegedly killed by gunmen in a ransom attempt.
  • Year: 1983 - A dust storm hits Melbourne, resulting in the worst drought on record and severe weather conditions in the city.
  • Year: 1974 - The crew of Skylab 4, the last mission to visit the American space station Skylab, returns to Earth after 84 days in space.
  • Year: 1971 - South Vietnamese ground troops launch an incursion into Laos to try to cut off the Ho Chi Minh trail and stop communist infiltration into the country.
  • Year: 1971 - The NASDAQ stock market index opens for the first time.
  • Year: 1968 - American civil rights movement: An attack on Black students from South Carolina State University who are protesting racial segregation leaves three dead and 28 injured in Orangeburg, South Carolina.
  • Year: 1965 - Eastern Air Lines Flight 663 crashes into the Atlantic Ocean and explodes, killing all 84 people onboard.
  • Year: 1963 - The regime of Prime Minister of Iraq Abd al-Karim Qasim is overthrown by the Ba'ath Party.
  • Year: 1962 - Nine protestors are killed at Charonne station, Paris, by French police under the command of ex-Vichy official and Parisian Prefect of Police Maurice Papon.
  • Year: 1960 - The Hollywood Walk of Fame is founded.
  • Year: 1960 - Queen Elizabeth II issues an Order-in-Council, proclaiming the House of Windsor and declaring that her descendants will take the name Mountbatten-Windsor.
  • Year: 1950 - The Stasi, the secret police of East Germany, is established.
  • Year: 1946 - The People's Republic of Korea is dissolved in the North and replaced by the communist-controlled Provisional People's Committee of North Korea.
  • Year: 1945 - World War II: Mikhail Devyataev escapes with nine other Soviet POWs from a Nazi concentration camp in Peenemünde, Usedom.
  • Year: 1945 - World War II: British and Canadian forces commence Operation Veritable to occupy land between the Maas and Rhine rivers.
  • Year: 1942 - World War II: Japan invades Singapore.
  • Year: 1937 - Spanish Civil War: Republican forces establish the Interprovincial Council of Santander, Palencia and Burgos in Cantabria.
  • Year: 1924 - The first state execution in the United States by gas chamber takes place in Nevada.
  • Year: 1915 - D. W. Griffith's controversial landmark film The Birth of a Nation premieres in Los Angeles.
  • Year: 1910 - The Boy Scouts of America is incorporated by William D. Boyce.
  • Year: 1904 - The Dutch Colonial Army's Marechaussee regiment led by General G.C.E. van Daalen launch a military campaign in the Dutch East Indies' Northern Sumatra region, leading to the deaths of thousands of civilians.
  • Year: 1904 - Japanese forces launch a surprise attack against Russian-controlled Port Arthur, marking the start of the Russo-Japanese war.
  • Year: 1887 - The Dawes Act is enacted, authorizing the U.S. President to divide Native American tribal land into individual allotments.
  • Year: 1885 - The first Japanese immigrants arrive in Hawaii.
  • Year: 1879 - England's cricket team, led by Lord Harris, is attacked in a riot during a match in Sydney.
  • Year: 1879 - Sandford Fleming first proposes the adoption of Universal Standard Time at a meeting of the Royal Canadian Institute.
  • Year: 1865 - Delaware refuses to ratify the Thirteenth Amendment to the U.S. Constitution, delaying the criminalization of slavery until the amendment's national adoption on December 6, 1865. The amendment is ultimately ratified by Delaware on February 12, 1901, the 92nd anniversary of Abraham Lincoln's birth.
  • Year: 1837 - Richard Johnson becomes the first and only Vice President of the United States chosen by the Senate.
  • Year: 1817 - An army led by Grand Marshal Las Heras crosses the Andes to join San Martín in the liberation of Chile from Spain.
  • Year: 1807 - Napoleon defeats the coalition forces of Russian General Bennigsen and Prussian General L'Estocq at the Battle of Eylau.
  • Year: 1693 - The College of William & Mary in Williamsburg, Virginia, the second-oldest institution of higher education in the Thirteen Colonies, is granted a charter by King William III and Queen Mary II.
  • Year: 1601 - Robert Devereux, 2nd Earl of Essex, unsuccessfully rebels against Queen Elizabeth I.
  • Year: 1587 - Mary, Queen of Scots is executed on suspicion of having been involved in the Babington Plot to murder her cousin, Queen Elizabeth I.
  • Year: 1347 - The Byzantine civil war of 1341-47 ends with a power-sharing agreement between John VI Kantakouzenos and John V Palaiologos.
  • Year: 1250 - Seventh Crusade: Crusaders engage Ayyubid forces in the Battle of Al Mansurah.
  • Year: 1238 - The Mongols burn the Russian city of Vladimir.
  • Year: 421 - Constantius III becomes co-emperor of the Western Roman Empire.
