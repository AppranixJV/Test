Historical Events for 04/12

  • Year: 2014 - The Great Fire of Valparaíso ravages the Chilean city of Valparaíso, killing 16 people, displacing nearly 10,000, and destroying over 2,000 homes.
  • Year: 2013 - Two suicide bombers kill three Chadian soldiers and injure dozens of civilians at a market in Kidal, Mali.
  • Year: 2010 - Merano derailment: A rail accident in South Tyrol kills nine people and injures a further 28.
  • Year: 2009 - Zimbabwe officially abandons the Zimbabwean dollar as its official currency.
  • Year: 2007 - A suicide bomber penetrates the Green Zone and detonates in a cafeteria within a parliament building, killing Iraqi MP Mohammed Awad and wounding more than twenty other people.
  • Year: 2002 - A suicide bomber blows herself up at the entrance to Jerusalem's Mahane Yehuda Market, killing seven people and wounding 104.
  • Year: 1999 - United States President Bill Clinton is cited for contempt of court for giving "intentionally false statements" in a civil lawsuit; he is later fined and disbarred.
  • Year: 1992 - The Euro Disney Resort officially opens with its theme park Euro Disneyland; the resort and its park's name are subsequently changed to Disneyland Paris.
  • Year: 1990 - Widerøe Flight 839 crashes after takeoff from Værøy Airport in Norway, killing five people.
  • Year: 1990 - Jim Gary's "Twentieth Century Dinosaurs" exhibition opens at the Smithsonian Institution National Museum of Natural History in Washington, D.C. He is the only sculptor ever invited to present a solo exhibition there.
  • Year: 1983 - Harold Washington is elected as the first black mayor of Chicago.
  • Year: 1981 - The first launch of a Space Shuttle (Columbia) takes place: The STS-1 mission.
  • Year: 1980 - Canadian runner and athlete, Terry Fox begins his Marathon of Hope Run in St. John's, NF
  • Year: 1980 - Transbrasil Flight 303, a Boeing 727, crashes on approach to Hercílio Luz International Airport, in Florianópolis, Brazil. Fifty-five out of the 58 people on board are killed.
  • Year: 1980 - The Americo-Liberian government of Liberia is violently deposed.
  • Year: 1970 - Soviet submarine K-8, carrying four nuclear torpedoes, sinks in the Bay of Biscay four days after a fire on board.
  • Year: 1963 - The Soviet nuclear-powered submarine K-33 collides with the Finnish merchant vessel M/S Finnclipper in the Danish straits.
  • Year: 1961 - Space Race: The Soviet cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin becomes the first human to travel into outer space and perform the first crewed orbital flight, Vostok 1.
  • Year: 1955 - The polio vaccine, developed by Dr. Jonas Salk, is declared safe and effective.
  • Year: 1945 - World War II: The U.S. Ninth Army under General William H. Simpson crosses the Elbe River astride Magdeburg, and reaches Tangermünde—only 50 miles from Berlin.
  • Year: 1945 - U.S. President Franklin D. Roosevelt dies in office; Vice President Harry S. Truman becomes President upon Roosevelt's death.
  • Year: 1937 - Sir Frank Whittle ground-tests the first jet engine designed to power an aircraft, at Rugby, England.
  • Year: 1934 - The U.S. Auto-Lite strike begins, culminating in a five-day melee between Ohio National Guard troops and 6,000 strikers and picketers.
  • Year: 1934 - The strongest surface wind gust in the world at the time of 231 mph, is measured on the summit of Mount Washington, New Hampshire. It has since been surpassed.
  • Year: 1928 - The Bremen, a German Junkers W 33 type aircraft, takes off for the first successful transatlantic aeroplane flight from east to west.
  • Year: 1927 - Rocksprings, Texas is hit by an F5 tornado that destroys 235 of the 247 buildings in the town, kills 72 townspeople and injures 205; third deadliest tornado in Texas history.
  • Year: 1927 - Shanghai massacre of 1927: Chiang Kai-shek orders the Chinese Communist Party members executed in Shanghai, ending the First United Front.
  • Year: 1917 - World War I: Canadian forces successfully complete the taking of Vimy Ridge from the Germans.
  • Year: 1910 - SMS Zrínyi, one of the last pre-dreadnought battleships built by the Austro-Hungarian Navy, is launched.
  • Year: 1900 - One day after its enactment by the Congress, President William McKinley signs the Foraker Act into law, giving Puerto Rico limited self-rule.
  • Year: 1877 - The United Kingdom annexes the Transvaal.
  • Year: 1865 - American Civil War: Mobile, Alabama, falls to the Union Army.
  • Year: 1864 - American Civil War: The Battle of Fort Pillow: Confederate forces kill most of the African American soldiers that surrendered at Fort Pillow, Tennessee.
  • Year: 1862 - American Civil War: The Andrews Raid (the Great Locomotive Chase) occurs, starting from Big Shanty, Georgia (now Kennesaw).
  • Year: 1861 - American Civil War: Battle of Fort Sumter. The war begins with Confederate forces firing on Fort Sumter, in the harbor of Charleston, South Carolina.
  • Year: 1831 - Soldiers marching on the Broughton Suspension Bridge in Manchester, England, cause it to collapse.
  • Year: 1820 - Alexander Ypsilantis is declared leader of Filiki Eteria, a secret organization to overthrow Ottoman rule over Greece.
  • Year: 1807 - The Froberg mutiny on Malta ends when the remaining mutineers blow up the magazine of Fort Ricasoli.
  • Year: 1776 - American Revolution: With the Halifax Resolves, the North Carolina Provincial Congress authorizes its Congressional delegation to vote for independence from Britain.
  • Year: 1606 - The Union Flag is adopted as the flag of English and Scottish ships.
  • Year: 1204 - The Crusaders of the Fourth Crusade breach the walls of Constantinople and enter the city, which they completely occupy the following day.
  • Year: 1012 - Duke Oldřich of Bohemia deposes and blinds his brother Jaromír, who flees to Poland.
  • Year: 627 - King Edwin of Northumbria is converted to Christianity by Paulinus, bishop of York.
  • Year: 467 - Anthemius is elevated to Emperor of the Western Roman Empire.
  • Year: 240 - Shapur I becomes co-emperor of the Sasanian Empire with his father Ardashir I.
