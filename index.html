Historical Events for 11/15

  • Year: 2022 - The world population reached 8 billion.
  • Year: 2020 - Lewis Hamilton wins the Turkish Grand Prix and secures his seventh drivers' title, equalling the all-time record held by Michael Schumacher.
  • Year: 2016 - Hong Kong's High Court bans elected politicians Yau Wai-ching and Baggio Leung from the city's Parliament.
  • Year: 2013 - Sony releases the PlayStation 4 (PS4) game console.
  • Year: 2012 - Xi Jinping becomes General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party and a new seven-member Politburo Standing Committee is inaugurated.
  • Year: 2007 - Cyclone Sidr hits Bangladesh, killing an estimated 5,000 people and destroying parts of the world's largest mangrove forest, the Sundarbans.
  • Year: 2006 - Al Jazeera English launches worldwide.
  • Year: 2003 - The first day of the 2003 Istanbul bombings, in which two car bombs, targeting two synagogues, explode, kill 25 people and wound 300 more.
  • Year: 2002 - Hu Jintao becomes General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party and a new nine-member Politburo Standing Committee is inaugurated.
  • Year: 2001 - Microsoft launches the Xbox game console.
  • Year: 2000 - Jharkhand officially becomes the 28th state of India, formed from eighteen districts of southern Bihar.
  • Year: 2000 - A chartered Antonov An-24 crashes after takeoff from Luanda, Angola, killing more than 40 people.
  • Year: 1994 - A magnitude 7.1 earthquake hits the central Philippine island of Mindoro, killing 78 people, injuring 430 and triggering a tsunami up to 8.5 m (28 ft) high.
  • Year: 1990 - The Communist People's Republic of Bulgaria is disestablished and a new republican government is instituted.
  • Year: 1988 - The first Fairtrade label, Max Havelaar, is launched in the Netherlands.
  • Year: 1988 - Israeli-Palestinian conflict: An independent State of Palestine is proclaimed by the Palestinian National Council.
  • Year: 1988 - In the Soviet Union, the uncrewed Shuttle Buran makes its only space flight.
  • Year: 1987 - In Brașov, Romania, workers rebel against the communist regime of Nicolae Ceaușescu.
  • Year: 1985 - The Anglo-Irish Agreement is signed at Hillsborough Castle by British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher and Irish Taoiseach Garret FitzGerald.
  • Year: 1985 - A research assistant is injured when a package from the Unabomber addressed to a University of Michigan professor explodes.
  • Year: 1983 - Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus declares independence; it is only recognized by Turkey.
  • Year: 1979 - A package from Unabomber Ted Kaczynski begins smoking in the cargo hold of a flight from Chicago to Washington, D.C., forcing the plane to make an emergency landing.
  • Year: 1978 - A chartered Douglas DC-8 crashes near Colombo, Sri Lanka, killing 183.
  • Year: 1976 - René Lévesque and the Parti Québécois take power to become the first Quebec government of the 20th century clearly in favor of independence.
  • Year: 1971 - Intel releases the world's first commercial single-chip microprocessor, the 4004.
  • Year: 1969 - Vietnam War: In Washington, D.C., 250,000-500,000 protesters staged a peaceful demonstration against the war, including a symbolic "March Against Death".
  • Year: 1969 - Cold War: The Soviet submarine K-19 collides with the American submarine USS Gato in the Barents Sea.
  • Year: 1968 - The Cleveland Transit System becomes the first transit system in the western hemisphere to provide direct rapid transit service from a city's downtown to its major airport.
  • Year: 1967 - The only fatality of the North American X-15 program occurs during the 191st flight when Air Force test pilot Michael J. Adams loses control of his aircraft which is destroyed mid-air over the Mojave Desert.
  • Year: 1966 - Pan Am Flight 708 crashes near Dallgow-Döberitz, East Germany, killing all three people on board.
  • Year: 1966 - Project Gemini: Gemini 12 completes the program's final mission, when it splashes down safely in the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Year: 1965 - Craig Breedlove sets a land speed record of 600.601 mph (966.574 km/h) in his car, the Spirit of America, at the Bonneville Salt Flats in Utah.
  • Year: 1955 - The first part of the Saint Petersburg Metro is opened.
  • Year: 1951 - Greek resistance leader Nikos Beloyannis, along with 11 other resistance members, is sentenced to death by the court-martial.
  • Year: 1943 - The Holocaust: German SS leader Heinrich Himmler orders that Gypsies are to be put "on the same level as Jews and placed in concentration camps".
  • Year: 1942 - World War II: The Battle of Guadalcanal ends in a decisive Allied victory.
  • Year: 1938 - Nazi Germany bans Jewish children from public schools in the aftermath of Kristallnacht.
  • Year: 1933 - Thailand holds its first election.
  • Year: 1928 - The RNLI lifeboat Mary Stanford capsizes in Rye Harbour with the loss of the entire 17-man crew.
  • Year: 1922 - At least 300 are massacred during a general strike in Guayaquil, Ecuador.
  • Year: 1920 - The Free City of Danzig is established.
  • Year: 1920 - The first assembly of the League of Nations is held in Geneva, Switzerland.
  • Year: 1917 - Eduskunta declares itself the supreme state power of Finland, prompting its declaration of independence and secession from Russia.
  • Year: 1889 - Brazil is declared a republic by Marshal Deodoro da Fonseca as Emperor Pedro II is deposed in a military coup.
  • Year: 1864 - American Civil War: Union General William Tecumseh Sherman begins his March to the Sea.
  • Year: 1849 - Boilers of the steamboat Louisiana explode as she pulls back from the dock in New Orleans, killing more than 150 people.
  • Year: 1806 - Pike Expedition: Lieutenant Zebulon Pike spots a mountain peak while near the Colorado foothills of the Rocky Mountains. It is later named Pikes Peak in his honor.
  • Year: 1777 - American Revolutionary War: After 16 months of debate the Continental Congress approves the Articles of Confederation.
  • Year: 1760 - The secondly-built Castellania in Valletta is officially inaugurated with the blessing of the interior Chapel of Sorrows.
  • Year: 1705 - Rákóczi's War of Independence: The Habsburg Empire and Denmark win a military victory over the Kurucs from Hungary in the Battle of Zsibó.
  • Year: 1533 - Francisco Pizarro arrives in Cuzco, the capital of the Inca Empire.
  • Year: 1532 - Spanish conquest of the Inca Empire: Commanded by Francisco Pizarro, Spanish conquistadors under Hernando de Soto meet Incan Emperor Atahualpa for the first time outside Cajamarca, arranging for a meeting in the city plaza the following day.
  • Year: 1315 - Growth of the Old Swiss Confederacy: The Schweizer Eidgenossenschaft ambushes the army of Leopold I in the Battle of Morgarten.
  • Year: 655 - Battle of the Winwaed: Penda of Mercia is defeated by Oswiu of Northumbria.
