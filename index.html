Historical Events for 11/27

  • Year: 2020 - Days after the announcement of its discovery, the Utah monolith is removed by recreationists.
  • Year: 2020 - Iran's top nuclear scientist, Mohsen Fakhrizadeh, is assassinated near Tehran.
  • Year: 2015 - An active shooter inside a Planned Parenthood facility in Colorado Springs, Colorado, shoots at least four police officers. One officer later dies. Two civilians are also killed, and six injured. The shooter later surrendered.
  • Year: 2009 - Nevsky Express bombing: A bomb explodes on the Nevsky Express train between Moscow and Saint Petersburg, derailing it and causing 28 deaths and 96 injuries.
  • Year: 2008 - XL Airways Germany Flight 888T: An Airbus A320 performing a flight test crashes near the French commune of Canet-en-Roussillon, killing all seven people on board.
  • Year: 2006 - The House of Commons of Canada approves a motion introduced by Prime Minister Stephen Harper recognizing the Québécois as a nation within Canada.
  • Year: 2004 - Pope John Paul II returns the relics of Saint John Chrysostom to the Eastern Orthodox Church.
  • Year: 2001 - A hydrogen atmosphere is discovered on the extrasolar planet Osiris by the Hubble Space Telescope, the first atmosphere detected on an extrasolar planet.
  • Year: 1999 - The centre-left Labour Party takes control of the New Zealand government with leader Helen Clark becoming the first elected female Prime Minister in New Zealand's history.
  • Year: 1997 - Twenty-five people are killed in the second Souhane massacre in Algeria.
  • Year: 1992 - For the second time in a year, military forces try to overthrow president Carlos Andrés Pérez in Venezuela.
  • Year: 1989 - Avianca Flight 203: A Boeing 727 explodes in mid-air over Colombia, killing all 107 people on board and three people on the ground. The Medellín Cartel will claim responsibility for the attack.
  • Year: 1984 - Under the Brussels Agreement signed between the governments of the United Kingdom and Spain, the former agrees to enter into discussions with Spain over Gibraltar, including sovereignty.
  • Year: 1983 - Avianca Flight 011: A Boeing 747 crashes near Madrid's Barajas Airport, killing 181.
  • Year: 1978 - The Kurdistan Workers' Party (PKK) is founded in the Turkish village of Fis.
  • Year: 1978 - In San Francisco, city mayor George Moscone and openly gay city supervisor Harvey Milk are assassinated by former supervisor Dan White.
  • Year: 1975 - The Provisional IRA assassinates Ross McWhirter, after a press conference in which McWhirter had announced a reward for the capture of those responsible for multiple bombings and shootings across England.
  • Year: 1973 - Twenty-fifth Amendment: The United States Senate votes 92-3 to confirm Gerald Ford as Vice President of the United States. (On December 6, the House will confirm him 387-35).
  • Year: 1971 - The Soviet space program's Mars 2 orbiter releases a descent module. It malfunctions and crashes, but it is the first man-made object to reach the surface of Mars.
  • Year: 1968 - Penny Ann Early becomes the first woman to play major professional basketball for the Kentucky Colonels in an ABA game against the Los Angeles Stars.
  • Year: 1965 - Vietnam War: The Pentagon tells U.S. President Lyndon B. Johnson that if planned operations are to succeed, the number of American troops in Vietnam has to be increased from 120,000 to 400,000.
  • Year: 1954 - Alger Hiss is released from prison after serving 44 months for perjury.
  • Year: 1945 - CARE (then the Cooperative for American Remittances to Europe) is founded to send CARE Packages of food relief to Europe after World War II.
  • Year: 1944 - World War II: RAF Fauld explosion: An explosion at a Royal Air Force ammunition dump in Staffordshire kills seventy people.
  • Year: 1942 - World War II: At Toulon, the French navy scuttles its ships and submarines to keep them out of Nazi hands.
  • Year: 1940 - World War II: At the Battle of Cape Spartivento, the Royal Navy engages the Regia Marina in the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Year: 1940 - In Romania, the ruling Iron Guard fascist party assassinates over 60 of arrested King Carol II of Romania's aides and other political dissidents.
  • Year: 1924 - In New York City, the first Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade is held.
  • Year: 1918 - The Makhnovshchina is established.
  • Year: 1917 - P. E. Svinhufvud becomes the chairman of his first senate, technically the first Prime Minister of Finland.
  • Year: 1912 - Spain declares a protectorate over the north shore of Morocco.
  • Year: 1901 - The U.S. Army War College is established.
  • Year: 1896 - Also sprach Zarathustra by Richard Strauss is first performed.
  • Year: 1895 - At the Swedish-Norwegian Club in Paris, Alfred Nobel signs his last will and testament, setting aside his estate to establish the Nobel Prize after he dies.
  • Year: 1868 - American Indian Wars: Battle of Washita River: United States Army Lieutenant Colonel George Armstrong Custer leads an attack on Cheyenne living on reservation land.
  • Year: 1863 - American Civil War: Battle of Mine Run: Union forces under General George Meade take up positions against troops led by Confederate General Robert E. Lee.
  • Year: 1863 - American Civil War: Confederate cavalry leader John Hunt Morgan and several of his men escape the Ohio Penitentiary and return safely to the South.
  • Year: 1856 - The Coup of 1856 leads to Luxembourg's unilateral adoption of a new, reactionary constitution.
  • Year: 1839 - In Boston, Massachusetts, the American Statistical Association is founded.
  • Year: 1835 - James Pratt and John Smith are hanged in London; they are the last two to be executed for sodomy in England.
  • Year: 1830 - Saint Catherine Labouré experiences a Marian apparition.
  • Year: 1815 - Adoption of Constitution of the Kingdom of Poland.
  • Year: 1809 - The Berners Street hoax is perpetrated by Theodore Hook in the City of Westminster, London.
  • Year: 1727 - The foundation stone to the Jerusalem Church in Berlin is laid.
  • Year: 1542 - Palace plot of Renyin year: A group of Ming dynasty palace women fail to murder the Jiajing Emperor, and are executed by slow-slicing.
  • Year: 1095 - Pope Urban II declares the First Crusade at the Council of Clermont.
  • Year: 602 - Byzantine Emperor Maurice is forced to watch as the usurper Phocas executes his five sons before Maurice is beheaded himself.
  • Year: 511 - King Clovis I dies at Lutetia and is buried in the Abbey of St Genevieve.
  • Year: 395 - Rufinus, praetorian prefect of the East, is murdered by Gothic mercenaries under Gainas.
  • Year: 176 - Emperor Marcus Aurelius grants his son Commodus the rank of "Imperator" and makes him Supreme Commander of the Roman legions.
  • Year: 25 - Luoyang is declared capital of the Eastern Han dynasty by Emperor Guangwu of Han.
