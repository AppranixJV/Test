Historical Events for 03/18

  • Year: 2015 - The Bardo National Museum in Tunisia is attacked by gunmen. Twenty-three people, almost all tourists, are killed, and at least 50 other people are wounded.
  • Year: 2014 - The parliaments of Russia and Crimea sign an accession treaty.
  • Year: 1997 - The tail of a Russian Antonov An-24 charter plane breaks off while en route to Turkey, causing the plane to crash and killing all 50 people on board.
  • Year: 1996 - A nightclub fire in Quezon City, Philippines kills 162 people.
  • Year: 1994 - Bosnia's Bosniaks and Croats sign the Washington Agreement, ending war between the Croatian Republic of Herzeg-Bosnia and the Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina, and establishing the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina.
  • Year: 1990 - In the largest art theft in US history, 12 paintings, collectively worth around $500 million, are stolen from the Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum in Boston.
  • Year: 1990 - Germans in the German Democratic Republic vote in the first democratic elections in the former communist dictatorship.
  • Year: 1980 - A Vostok-2M rocket at Plesetsk Cosmodrome Site 43 explodes during a fueling operation, killing 48 people.
  • Year: 1974 - Güzel İstanbul, a nude sculpture by Gürdal Duyar in Istanbul is torn down in the middle of the night
  • Year: 1971 - Peru: A landslide crashes into Yanawayin Lake, killing 200 people at the mining camp of Chungar.
  • Year: 1970 - Lon Nol ousts Prince Norodom Sihanouk of Cambodia.
  • Year: 1969 - The United States begins secretly bombing the Sihanouk Trail in Cambodia, used by communist forces to infiltrate South Vietnam.
  • Year: 1968 - Gold standard: The U.S. Congress repeals the requirement for a gold reserve to back US currency.
  • Year: 1967 - The supertanker Torrey Canyon runs aground off the Cornish coast.
  • Year: 1966 - United Arab Airlines Flight 749 crashes on approach to Cairo International Airport in Cairo, Egypt, killing 30 people.
  • Year: 1965 - Cosmonaut Alexei Leonov, leaving his spacecraft Voskhod 2 for 12 minutes, becomes the first person to walk in space.
  • Year: 1962 - The Évian Accords end the Algerian War of Independence, which had begun in 1954.
  • Year: 1959 - The Hawaii Admission Act is signed into law.
  • Year: 1953 - An earthquake hits western Turkey, killing at least 1,070 people.
  • Year: 1948 - Soviet consultants leave Yugoslavia in the first sign of the Tito-Stalin Split.
  • Year: 1944 - Mount Vesuvius in Italy erupts, killing 26 people, causing thousands to flee their homes, and destroying dozens of Allied bombers.
  • Year: 1942 - The War Relocation Authority is established in the United States to take Japanese Americans into custody.
  • Year: 1940 - World War II: Adolf Hitler and Benito Mussolini meet at the Brenner Pass in the Alps and agree to form an alliance against France and the United Kingdom.
  • Year: 1938 - Mexico creates Pemex by expropriating all foreign-owned oil reserves and facilities.
  • Year: 1937 - Spanish Civil War: Spanish Republican forces defeat the Italians at the Battle of Guadalajara.
  • Year: 1937 - The New London School explosion in New London, Texas, kills 300 people, mostly children.
  • Year: 1925 - The Tri-State Tornado hits the Midwestern states of Missouri, Illinois, and Indiana, killing 695 people.
  • Year: 1922 - In India, Mohandas Gandhi is sentenced to six years in prison for civil disobedience, of which he serves only two.
  • Year: 1921 - Mongolian Revolution of 1921: The Mongolian People's Army defeated local Chinese forces at Altanbulag, Selenge (then known as Maimachen). This battle was seen as the birthday of the People's Army and completed the expulsion of Chinese militants in Mongolia.
  • Year: 1921 - The Kronstadt rebellion is suppressed by the Red Army.
  • Year: 1921 - The second Peace of Riga is signed between Poland and the Soviet Union.
  • Year: 1915 - World War I: During the Battle of Gallipoli, three battleships are sunk during a failed British and French naval attack on the Dardanelles.
  • Year: 1913 - King George I of Greece is assassinated in the recently liberated city of Thessaloniki.
  • Year: 1902 - Macario Sakay issues Presidential Order No. 1 of his Tagalog Republic.
  • Year: 1901 - The Kumasi Mutiny of 1901 begins.
  • Year: 1899 - Phoebe, a satellite of Saturn, becomes the first to be discovered with photographs, taken in August 1898, by William Henry Pickering.
  • Year: 1874 - The Hawaiian Kingdom signs a treaty with the United States granting exclusive trade rights.
  • Year: 1871 - Declaration of the Paris Commune; President of the French Republic, Adolphe Thiers, orders the evacuation of Paris.
  • Year: 1865 - American Civil War: The Congress of the Confederate States adjourns for the last time.
  • Year: 1848 - Revolutions of 1848: A rebellion arose in Milan which in five days of street fighting drove Marshal Radetzky and his Austrian soldiers from the city.
  • Year: 1848 - The premiere of Fry's Leonora in Philadelphia is the first known performance of an grand opera by an American composer.
  • Year: 1834 - Six farm labourers from Tolpuddle, Dorset, England are sentenced to be transported to Australia for forming a trade union.
  • Year: 1793 - Flanders Campaign of the French Revolution, Battle of Neerwinden.
  • Year: 1793 - The first modern republic in Germany, the Republic of Mainz, is declared by Andreas Joseph Hofmann.
  • Year: 1766 - American Revolution: The British Parliament repeals the Stamp Act.
  • Year: 1741 - New York governor George Clarke's complex at Fort George is burned in an arson attack, starting the New York Conspiracy of 1741.
  • Year: 1673 - English lord John Berkeley sold his half of New Jersey to the Quakers
  • Year: 1644 - The Third Anglo-Powhatan War begins in the Colony of Virginia.
  • Year: 1608 - Susenyos is formally crowned Emperor of Ethiopia.
  • Year: 1571 - Valletta is made the capital city of Malta.
  • Year: 1438 - Albert II of Habsburg becomes King of the Romans.
  • Year: 1314 - Jacques de Molay, the 23rd and final Grand Master of the Knights Templar, is burned at the stake.
  • Year: 1241 - First Mongol invasion of Poland: Mongols overwhelm Polish armies in Kraków in the Battle of Chmielnik and plunder the city.
  • Year: 1229 - Frederick II, Holy Roman Emperor, declares himself King of Jerusalem in the Sixth Crusade.
  • Year: 1068 - An earthquake in the Levant and the Arabian Peninsula leaves up to 20,000 dead.
  • Year: 37 - Roman Senate annuls Tiberius' will and proclaims Gaius Julius Caesar Augustus Germanicus (aka Caligula = Little Boots) emperor.
