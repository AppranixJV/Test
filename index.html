Historical Events for 04/01

  • Year: 2016 - The 2016 Nagorno-Karabakh conflict begins along the Nagorno-Karabakh Line of Contact.
  • Year: 2011 - After protests against the burning of the Quran turn violent, a mob attacks a United Nations compound in Mazar-i-Sharif, Afghanistan, resulting in the deaths of thirteen people, including eight foreign workers.
  • Year: 2006 - Serious Organised Crime Agency (SOCA) of the Government of the United Kingdom is enforced, but later merged into National Crime Agency on 7 October 2013.
  • Year: 2004 - Google launches its Email service Gmail.
  • Year: 2001 - Same-sex marriage becomes legal in the Netherlands, the first contemporary country to allow it.
  • Year: 2001 - Former President of Federal Republic of Yugoslavia Slobodan Milošević surrenders to police special forces, to be tried on war crimes charges.
  • Year: 2001 - An EP-3E United States Navy surveillance aircraft collides with a Chinese People's Liberation Army Shenyang J-8 fighter jet. The Chinese pilot ejected but is subsequently lost. The Navy crew makes an emergency landing in Hainan, China and is detained.
  • Year: 1999 - Nunavut is established as a Canadian territory carved out of the eastern part of the Northwest Territories.
  • Year: 1997 - Comet Hale-Bopp is seen passing at perihelion.
  • Year: 1993 - NASCAR racer Alan Kulwicki is killed in a plane crash near the Tri-Cities Regional Airport in Blountville, Tennessee.
  • Year: 1989 - Margaret Thatcher's new local government tax, the Community Charge (commonly known as the "poll tax"), is introduced in Scotland.
  • Year: 1986 - Communist Party of Nepal (Mashal) cadres attack a number of police stations in Kathmandu, seeking to incite a popular rebellion.
  • Year: 1984 - Singer Marvin Gaye is shot to death by his father in his home in Arlington Heights, Los Angeles, California.
  • Year: 1979 - Iran becomes an Islamic republic by a 99% vote, officially overthrowing the Shah.
  • Year: 1976 - Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak found Apple Computer, Inc.
  • Year: 1974 - The Local Government Act 1972 of England and Wales comes into effect.
  • Year: 1973 - Project Tiger, a tiger conservation project, is launched in the Jim Corbett National Park, India.
  • Year: 1971 - Bangladesh Liberation War: The Pakistan Army massacre more than a thousand people in Keraniganj Upazila, Bangladesh.
  • Year: 1970 - A Royal Air Maroc Sud Aviation Caravelle crashes near Berrechid, Morocco, killing 61.
  • Year: 1970 - President Richard Nixon signs the Public Health Cigarette Smoking Act into law.
  • Year: 1969 - The Hawker Siddeley Harrier, the first operational fighter aircraft with Vertical/Short Takeoff and Landing capabilities, enters service with the Royal Air Force.
  • Year: 1964 - The British Admiralty, War Office and Air Ministry are replaced by a unified Defence Council of the United Kingdom.
  • Year: 1960 - The TIROS-1 satellite transmits the first television picture from space.
  • Year: 1955 - The EOKA rebellion against the British Empire begins in Cyprus, with the goal of unifying with Greece.
  • Year: 1954 - United States President Dwight D. Eisenhower authorizes the creation of the United States Air Force Academy in Colorado Springs, Colorado.
  • Year: 1949 - The Government of Canada repeals Japanese-Canadian internment after seven years.
  • Year: 1949 - Chinese Civil War: The Chinese Communist Party holds unsuccessful peace talks with the Nationalist Party in Beijing, after three years of fighting.
  • Year: 1948 - Faroe Islands gain autonomy from Denmark.
  • Year: 1948 - Cold War: Communist forces respond to the introduction of the Deutsche Mark by attempting to force the western powers to withdraw from Berlin.
  • Year: 1947 - The only mutiny in the history of the Royal New Zealand Navy begins.
  • Year: 1946 - The Malayan Union is established. Protests from locals led to the establishment of the Federation of Malaya two years later.
  • Year: 1946 - The 8.6 Mw  Aleutian Islands earthquake shakes the Aleutian Islands with a maximum Mercalli intensity of VI (Strong). A destructive tsunami reaches the Hawaiian Islands resulting in dozens of deaths, mostly in Hilo, Hawaii.
  • Year: 1945 - World War II: The Tenth United States Army attacks the Thirty-Second Japanese Army on Okinawa.
  • Year: 1944 - World War II: Navigation errors lead to an accidental American bombing of the Swiss city of Schaffhausen.
  • Year: 1941 - A military coup in Iraq overthrows the regime of 'Abd al-Ilah and installs Rashid Ali al-Gaylani as Prime Minister.
  • Year: 1941 - Fântâna Albă massacre: Between two hundred and two thousand Romanian civilians are killed by Soviet Border Troops.
  • Year: 1939 - Spanish Civil War: Generalísimo Francisco Franco of the Spanish State announces the end of the Spanish Civil War, when the last of the Republican forces surrender.
  • Year: 1937 - The Royal New Zealand Air Force is formed as an independent service.
  • Year: 1937 - Aden becomes a British crown colony.
  • Year: 1935 - India's central banking institution, the Reserve Bank of India, is formed.
  • Year: 1933 - The recently elected Nazis under Julius Streicher organize a one-day boycott of all Jewish-owned businesses in Germany, ushering in a series of anti-Semitic acts.
  • Year: 1924 - The Royal Canadian Air Force is formed.
  • Year: 1924 - Adolf Hitler is sentenced to five years imprisonment for his participation in the "Beer Hall Putsch" but spends only nine months in jail.
  • Year: 1918 - The Royal Air Force is created by the merger of the Royal Flying Corps and the Royal Naval Air Service.
  • Year: 1908 - The Territorial Force (renamed Territorial Army in 1920) is formed as a volunteer reserve component of the British Army.
  • Year: 1900 - Prince George becomes absolute monarch of the Cretan State.
  • Year: 1873 - The White Star steamer SS Atlantic sinks off Nova Scotia, killing 547 in one of the worst marine disasters of the 19th century.
  • Year: 1867 - Singapore becomes a British crown colony.
  • Year: 1865 - American Civil War: Union troops led by Philip Sheridan decisively defeat Confederate troops led by George Pickett, cutting the Army of Northern Virginia's last supply line during the Siege of Petersburg.
  • Year: 1833 - The Convention of 1833, a political gathering of settlers in Mexican Texas to help draft a series of petitions to the Mexican government, begins in San Felipe de Austin.
  • Year: 1789 - In New York City, the United States House of Representatives achieves its first quorum and elects Frederick Muhlenberg of Pennsylvania as its first Speaker.
  • Year: 1572 - In the Eighty Years' War, the Watergeuzen capture Brielle from the Seventeen Provinces, gaining the first foothold on land for what would become the Dutch Republic.
  • Year: 1081 - Alexios I Komnenos overthrows the Byzantine emperor Nikephoros III Botaneiates, and, after his troops spend three days extensively looting Constantinople, is formally crowned on April 4.
  • Year: 527 - Byzantine Emperor Justin I names his nephew Justinian I as co-ruler and successor to the throne.
