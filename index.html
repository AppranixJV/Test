Historical Events for 04/07

  • Year: 2022 - Ketanji Brown Jackson is confirmed for the Supreme Court of the United States, becoming the first black female justice.
  • Year: 2021 - COVID-19 pandemic: The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention announces that the SARS-CoV-2 Alpha variant has become the dominant strain of COVID-19 in the United States.
  • Year: 2020 - COVID-19 pandemic: Acting Secretary of the Navy Thomas Modly resigns for his handling of the COVID-19 pandemic on USS Theodore Roosevelt and the dismissal of Brett Crozier.
  • Year: 2020 - COVID-19 pandemic: China ends its lockdown in Wuhan.
  • Year: 2018 - Syria launches the Douma chemical attack during the Eastern Ghouta offensive of the Syrian Civil War.
  • Year: 2018 - Former Brazilian president, Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva, is arrested for corruption by determination of Judge Sérgio Moro, from the “Car-Wash Operation”. Lula stayed imprisoned for 580 days, after being released by the Brazilian Supreme Court.
  • Year: 2017 - U.S. President Donald Trump orders the 2017 Shayrat missile strike against Syria in retaliation for the Khan Shaykhun chemical attack.
  • Year: 2017 - A man deliberately drives a hijacked truck into a crowd of people in Stockholm, Sweden, killing five people and injuring fifteen others.
  • Year: 2011 - The Israel Defense Forces use their Iron Dome missile system to successfully intercept a BM-21 Grad launched from Gaza, marking the first short-range missile intercept ever.
  • Year: 2009 - Mass protests begin across Moldova under the belief that results from the parliamentary election are fraudulent.
  • Year: 2009 - Former Peruvian President Alberto Fujimori is sentenced to 25 years in prison for ordering killings and kidnappings by security forces.
  • Year: 2003 - Iraq War: U.S. troops capture Baghdad; Saddam Hussein's Ba'athist regime falls two days later.
  • Year: 2001 - NASA launches the 2001 Mars Odyssey orbiter.
  • Year: 1999 - Turkish Airlines Flight 5904 crashes near Ceyhan in southern Turkey, killing six people.
  • Year: 1995 - First Chechen War: Russian paramilitary troops begin a massacre of civilians in Samashki, Chechnya.
  • Year: 1994 - Auburn Calloway attempts to destroy Federal Express Flight 705 in order to allow his family to benefit from his life insurance policy.
  • Year: 1994 - Rwandan genocide: Massacres of Tutsis begin in Kigali, Rwanda, and soldiers kill the civilian Prime Minister Agathe Uwilingiyimana.
  • Year: 1990 - John Poindexter is convicted for his role in the Iran-Contra affair. In 1991 the convictions are reversed on appeal.
  • Year: 1990 - A fire breaks out on the passenger ferry Scandinavian Star, killing 159 people.
  • Year: 1989 - Soviet submarine Komsomolets sinks in the Barents Sea off the coast of Norway, killing 42 sailors.
  • Year: 1988 - Soviet Defense Minister Dmitry Yazov orders the Soviet withdrawal from Afghanistan.
  • Year: 1983 - During STS-6, astronauts Story Musgrave and Don Peterson perform the first Space Shuttle spacewalk.
  • Year: 1982 - Iranian Foreign Affairs Minister Sadegh Ghotbzadeh is arrested.
  • Year: 1980 - During the Iran hostage crisis, the United States severs relations with Iran.
  • Year: 1978 - Development of the neutron bomb is canceled by President Jimmy Carter.
  • Year: 1977 - German Federal prosecutor Siegfried Buback and his driver are shot by two Red Army Faction members while waiting at a red light.
  • Year: 1976 - Member of Parliament and suspected spy John Stonehouse resigns from the Labour Party after being arrested for faking his own death.
  • Year: 1972 - Vietnam War: Communist forces overrun the South Vietnamese town of Loc Ninh.
  • Year: 1971 - Vietnam War: President Richard Nixon announces his decision to quicken the pace of Vietnamization.
  • Year: 1969 - The Internet's symbolic birth date: Publication of RFC 1.
  • Year: 1968 - Two-time Formula One British World Champion Jim Clark dies in an accident during a Formula Two race in Hockenheim.
  • Year: 1965 - Representatives of the National Congress of American Indians testify before members of the US Senate in Washington, D.C. against the termination of the Colville tribe.
  • Year: 1964 - IBM announces the System/360.
  • Year: 1956 - Francoist Spain agrees to surrender its protectorate in Morocco.
  • Year: 1955 - Winston Churchill resigns as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom amid indications of failing health.
  • Year: 1954 - United States President Dwight D. Eisenhower gives his "domino theory" speech during a news conference.
  • Year: 1948 - The World Health Organization is established by the United Nations.
  • Year: 1946 - The Soviet Union annexes East Prussia as the Kaliningrad Oblast of the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic.
  • Year: 1945 - World War II: The Imperial Japanese Navy battleship Yamato, one of the two largest ever constructed, is sunk by United States Navy aircraft during Operation Ten-Go.
  • Year: 1943 - The National Football League makes helmets mandatory.
  • Year: 1943 - Ioannis Rallis becomes collaborationist Prime Minister of Greece during the Axis Occupation.
  • Year: 1943 - The Holocaust in Ukraine: In Terebovlia, Germans order 1,100 Jews to undress and march through the city to the nearby village of Plebanivka, where they are shot and buried in ditches.
  • Year: 1940 - Booker T. Washington becomes the first African American to be depicted on a United States postage stamp.
  • Year: 1939 - Benito Mussolini declares an Italian protectorate over Albania and forces King Zog I into exile.
  • Year: 1933 - Nazi Germany issues the Law for the Restoration of the Professional Civil Service banning Jews and political dissidents from civil service posts.
  • Year: 1933 - Prohibition in the United States is repealed for beer of no more than 3.2% alcohol by weight, eight months before the ratification of the Twenty-first Amendment to the United States Constitution. (Now celebrated as National Beer Day in the United States.)
  • Year: 1927 - AT&T engineer Herbert Ives transmits the first long-distance public television broadcast (from Washington, D.C., to New York City, displaying the image of Commerce Secretary Herbert Hoover).
  • Year: 1926 - Violet Gibson attempts to assassinate Italian Prime Minister Benito Mussolini.
  • Year: 1922 - Teapot Dome scandal: United States Secretary of the Interior Albert B. Fall leases federal petroleum reserves to private oil companies on excessively generous terms.
  • Year: 1906 - The Algeciras Conference gives France and Spain control over Morocco.
  • Year: 1906 - Mount Vesuvius erupts and devastates Naples.
  • Year: 1868 - Thomas D'Arcy McGee, one of the Canadian Fathers of Confederation, is assassinated by a Fenian activist.
  • Year: 1862 - American Civil War: The Union's Army of the Tennessee and the Army of the Ohio defeat the Confederate Army of Mississippi near Shiloh, Tennessee.
  • Year: 1831 - Pedro II becomes Emperor of Empire of Brazil.
  • Year: 1824 - The Mechanics' Institution is established in Manchester, England at the Bridgewater Arms hotel, as part of a national movement for the education of working men. The institute is the precursor to three Universities in the city: the University of Manchester, UMIST and the Metropolitan University of Manchester (MMU).
  • Year: 1805 - German composer Ludwig van Beethoven premieres his Third Symphony, at the Theater an der Wien in Vienna.
  • Year: 1805 - Lewis and Clark Expedition: The Corps of Discovery breaks camp among the Mandan tribe and resumes its journey West along the Missouri River.
  • Year: 1798 - The Mississippi Territory is organized from disputed territory claimed by both the United States and the Spanish Empire. It is expanded in 1804 and again in 1812.
  • Year: 1790 - Greek War of Independence: Greek revolutionary Lambros Katsonis loses three of his ships in the Battle of Andros.
  • Year: 1795 - The French First Republic adopts the kilogram and gram as its primary unit of mass.
  • Year: 1788 - Settlers establish Marietta, Ohio, the first permanent settlement created by U.S. citizens in the recently organized Northwest Territory.
  • Year: 1767 - End of Burmese-Siamese War (1765-67).
  • Year: 1724 - Premiere performance of Johann Sebastian Bach's St John Passion, BWV 245, at St. Nicholas Church, Leipzig.
  • Year: 1541 - Francis Xavier leaves Lisbon on a mission to the Portuguese East Indies.
  • Year: 1521 - Ferdinand Magellan arrives at Cebu.
  • Year: 1449 - Felix V abdicates his claim to the papacy, ending the reign of the final Antipope.
  • Year: 1348 - Holy Roman Emperor Charles IV charters Prague University.
  • Year: 1141 - Empress Matilda becomes the first female ruler of England, adopting the title "Lady of the English".
  • Year: 529 - First Corpus Juris Civilis, a fundamental work in jurisprudence, is issued by Eastern Roman Emperor Justinian I.
  • Year: 451 - Attila the Hun captures Metz in France, killing most of its inhabitants and burning the town.
