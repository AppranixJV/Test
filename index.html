Historical Events for 10/05

  • Year: 2011 - In the Mekong River massacre, two Chinese cargo boats are hijacked and 13 crew members murdered.
  • Year: 2000 - Mass demonstrations in Serbia force the resignation of Slobodan Milošević.
  • Year: 1999 - The Ladbroke Grove rail crash in West London kills 31 people.
  • Year: 1991 - An Indonesian Air Force C-130 crash kills 135 people.
  • Year: 1990 - After 150 years The Herald newspaper in Melbourne, Australia, is published for the last time as a separate newspaper.
  • Year: 1988 - A Chilean opposition coalition defeats Augusto Pinochet in his re-election attempt.
  • Year: 1986 - Mordechai Vanunu's story in The Sunday Times reveals Israel's secret nuclear weapons.
  • Year: 1984 - Marc Garneau becomes the first Canadian in space.
  • Year: 1982 - Tylenol products are recalled after bottles in Chicago laced with cyanide cause seven deaths.
  • Year: 1974 - Bombs planted by the PIRA in pubs in Guildford kill four British soldiers and one civilian.
  • Year: 1970 - The British Trade Commissioner, James Cross, is kidnapped by members of the Front de libération du Québec, triggering the October Crisis in Canada.
  • Year: 1970 - The Public Broadcasting Service (PBS) is founded.
  • Year: 1968 - A Northern Ireland Civil Rights Association march in Derry is violently suppressed by police.
  • Year: 1966 - A reactor at the Enrico Fermi Nuclear Generating Station near Detroit suffers a partial meltdown.
  • Year: 1963 - The United States suspends the Commercial Import Program in response to repression of the Buddhist majority by the regime of President Ngo Dinh Diem.
  • Year: 1962 - The first Beatles single "Love Me Do" is released in Britain.
  • Year: 1962 - The first of the James Bond film series, based on the novels by Ian Fleming, Dr. No, is released in Britain.
  • Year: 1947 - President Truman makes the first televised Oval Office address.
  • Year: 1945 - A six-month strike by Hollywood set decorators turns into a bloody riot at the gates of the Warner Brothers studio.
  • Year: 1944 - The Provisional Government of the French Republic enfranchises women.
  • Year: 1943 - Ninety-eight American POWs are executed by Japanese forces on Wake Island.
  • Year: 1938 - In Nazi Germany, Jews' passports are invalidated.
  • Year: 1936 - The Jarrow March sets off for London.
  • Year: 1930 - British airship R101 crashes in France en route to India on its maiden voyage killing 48 people.
  • Year: 1921 - The World Series is the first to be broadcast on radio.
  • Year: 1914 - World War I: An aircraft successfully destroys another aircraft with gunfire for the first time.
  • Year: 1911 - The Kowloon-Canton Railway commences service.
  • Year: 1910 - In a revolution in Portugal the monarchy is overthrown and a republic is declared.
  • Year: 1905 - The Wright brothers pilot the Wright Flyer III in a new world record flight of 24 miles in 39 minutes.
  • Year: 1900 - Peace congress in Paris condemns British policy in South Africa and asserts Boer Republic's right to self-determination.
  • Year: 1877 - The Nez Perce War in the northwestern United States comes to an end.
  • Year: 1869 - The Eastman tunnel, in Minnesota, United States, collapses during construction, causing a landslide that nearly destroys St. Anthony Falls.
  • Year: 1869 - The Saxby Gale devastates the Bay of Fundy region in Canada.
  • Year: 1838 - The Killough massacre in east Texas sees eighteen Texian settlers either killed or kidnapped.
  • Year: 1813 - War of 1812: The Army of the Northwest defeats a British and Native Canadian force threatening Detroit.
  • Year: 1789 - French Revolution: The Women's March on Versailles effectively terminates royal authority.
  • Year: 1607 - Assassins attempt to kill Venetian statesman and scientist Paolo Sarpi.
  • Year: 1450 - Louis IX, Duke of Bavaria expels Jews from his jurisdiction.
  • Year: 1143 - With the signing of the Treaty of Zamora, King Alfonso VII of León and Castile recognises Portugal as a Kingdom.
  • Year: 869 - The Fourth Council of Constantinople is convened to depose patriarch Photios I.
  • Year: 816 - King Louis the Pious is crowned emperor of the Holy Roman Empire by the Pope.
  • Year: 610 - Heraclius arrives at Constantinople, kills Byzantine Emperor Phocas, and becomes emperor.
