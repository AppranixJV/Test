Historical Events for 03/31

  • Year: 2023 - An Historical Tornado outbreak occurs in the Midwest and the northern South
  • Year: 2018 - Start of the 2018 Armenian revolution.
  • Year: 2016 - NASA astronaut Scott Kelly and Roscosmos cosmonaut Mikhail Kornienko return to Earth after a yearlong mission at the International Space Station.
  • Year: 2004 - Iraq War in Anbar Province: In Fallujah, Iraq, four American private military contractors working for Blackwater USA, are killed after being ambushed.
  • Year: 1998 - Netscape releases Mozilla source code under an open source license.
  • Year: 1995 - TAROM Flight 371, an Airbus A310-300, crashes near Balotesti, Romania, killing all 60 people on board.
  • Year: 1995 - Selena is murdered by her fan club president Yolanda Saldívar at a Days Inn in Corpus Christi, Texas.
  • Year: 1993 - The Macao Basic Law is adopted by the Eighth National People's Congress of China to take effect December 20, 1999. Resumption by China of the Exercise of Sovereignty over Macao
  • Year: 1992 - The Treaty of Federation is signed in Moscow.
  • Year: 1992 - The USS Missouri, the last active United States Navy battleship, is decommissioned in Long Beach, California.
  • Year: 1991 - The Warsaw Pact formally disbands.
  • Year: 1991 - Georgian independence referendum: Nearly 99 percent of the voters support the country's independence from the Soviet Union.
  • Year: 1990 - Approximately 200,000 protesters take to the streets of London to protest against the newly introduced Poll Tax.
  • Year: 1986 - Mexicana de Aviación Flight 940 crashes into the Sierra Madre Oriental mountain range near the Mexican town of Maravatío, killing 167.
  • Year: 1980 - The Chicago, Rock Island and Pacific Railroad operates its final train after being ordered to liquidate its assets because of bankruptcy and debts owed to creditors.
  • Year: 1970 - Explorer 1 re-enters the Earth's atmosphere after 12 years in orbit.
  • Year: 1968 - American President Lyndon B. Johnson speaks to the nation of "Steps to Limit the War in Vietnam" in a television address. At the conclusion of his speech, he announces: "I shall not seek, and I will not accept, the nomination of my party for another term as your President."
  • Year: 1966 - The Labour Party under Harold Wilson wins the 1966 United Kingdom general election.
  • Year: 1966 - The Soviet Union launches Luna 10 which later becomes the first space probe to enter orbit around the Moon.
  • Year: 1964 - Brazilian General Olímpio Mourão Filho orders his troops to move towards Rio de Janeiro, beginning the coup d'état and 21 years of military dictatorship.
  • Year: 1959 - The 14th Dalai Lama, crosses the border into India and is granted political asylum.
  • Year: 1958 - In the Canadian federal election, the Progressive Conservatives, led by John Diefenbaker, win the largest percentage of seats in Canadian history, with 208 seats of 265.
  • Year: 1957 - Elections to the Territorial Assembly of the French colony Upper Volta are held. After the elections PDU and MDV form a government.
  • Year: 1951 - Remington Rand delivers the first UNIVAC I computer to the United States Census Bureau.
  • Year: 1949 - The Dominion of Newfoundland joins the Canadian Confederation and becomes the 10th Province of Canada.
  • Year: 1945 - World War II: A defecting German pilot delivers a Messerschmitt Me 262A-1, the world's first operational jet-powered fighter aircraft, to the Americans, the first to fall into Allied hands.
  • Year: 1942 - World War II: Japanese forces invade Christmas Island, then a British possession.
  • Year: 1939 - Events preceding World War II in Europe: Prime Minister Neville Chamberlain pledges British military support to the Second Polish Republic in the event of an invasion by Nazi Germany.
  • Year: 1933 - The Civilian Conservation Corps is established with the mission of relieving rampant unemployment in the United States.
  • Year: 1931 - A Transcontinental & Western Air airliner crashes near Bazaar, Kansas, killing eight, including University of Notre Dame head football coach Knute Rockne.
  • Year: 1931 - An earthquake in Nicaragua destroys Managua; killing 2,000.
  • Year: 1930 - The Motion Picture Production Code is instituted, imposing strict guidelines on the treatment of sex, crime, religion and violence in film, in the U.S., for the next thirty-eight years.
  • Year: 1921 - The Royal Australian Air Force is formed.
  • Year: 1918 - Daylight saving time goes into effect in the United States for the first time.
  • Year: 1918 - Massacre of ethnic Azerbaijanis is committed by allied armed groups of Armenian Revolutionary Federation and Bolsheviks. Nearly 12,000 Azerbaijani Muslims are killed.
  • Year: 1917 - According to the terms of the Treaty of the Danish West Indies, the islands become American possessions.
  • Year: 1913 - The Vienna Concert Society rioted during a performance of modernist music by Arnold Schoenberg, Alban Berg, Alexander von Zemlinsky, and Anton von Webern, causing a premature end to the concert due to violence; this concert became known as the Skandalkonzert.
  • Year: 1909 - Serbia formally withdraws its opposition to Austro-Hungarian actions in the Bosnian Crisis.
  • Year: 1906 - The Intercollegiate Athletic Association of the United States (later the National Collegiate Athletic Association) is established to set rules for college sports in the United States.
  • Year: 1905 - Kaiser Wilhelm II of Germany declares his support for Moroccan independence in Tangier, beginning the First Moroccan Crisis.
  • Year: 1901 - Rusalka by Antonín Dvořák premieres at the National Opera House in Prague.
  • Year: 1899 - Malolos, capital of the First Philippine Republic, is captured by American forces.
  • Year: 1889 - The Eiffel Tower is officially opened.
  • Year: 1885 - The United Kingdom establishes the Bechuanaland Protectorate.
  • Year: 1854 - Commodore Matthew Perry signs the Convention of Kanagawa with the Tokugawa Shogunate, opening the ports of Shimoda and Hakodate to American trade.
  • Year: 1814 - The Sixth Coalition occupies Paris after Napoleon's Grande Armée capitulates.
  • Year: 1774 - American Revolution: The Kingdom of Great Britain orders the port of Boston, Massachusetts closed pursuant to the Boston Port Act.
  • Year: 1761 - The 1761 Lisbon earthquake strikes off the Iberian Peninsula with an estimated magnitude of 8.5, six years after another quake destroyed the city.
  • Year: 1717 - A sermon on "The Nature of the Kingdom of Christ" by Benjamin Hoadly, the Bishop of Bangor, preached in the presence of King George I of Great Britain, provokes the Bangorian Controversy.
  • Year: 1657 - The Long Parliament presents the Humble Petition and Advice offering Oliver Cromwell the British throne, which he eventually declines.
  • Year: 1521 - Ferdinand Magellan and fifty of his men came ashore to present-day Limasawa to participate in the first Catholic mass in the Philippines.
  • Year: 1492 - Queen Isabella of Castile issues the Alhambra Decree, ordering her 150,000 Jewish and Muslim subjects to convert to Christianity or face expulsion.
  • Year: 1146 - Bernard of Clairvaux preaches his famous sermon in a field at Vézelay, urging the necessity of a Second Crusade. Louis VII is present, and joins the Crusade.
  • Year: 307 - After divorcing his wife Minervina, Constantine marries Fausta, daughter of the retired Roman emperor Maximian.
