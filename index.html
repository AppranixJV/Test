Historical Events for 03/13

  • Year: 2020 - Breonna Taylor is killed by police officers who were forcibly entering her home in Louisville, Kentucky; her death sparked extensive protests against racism and police brutality.
  • Year: 2020 - President Donald Trump declares the COVID-19 pandemic to be a national emergency in the United States.
  • Year: 2016 - Three gunmen attack two hotels in the Ivory Coast town of Grand-Bassam, killing at least 19 people.
  • Year: 2016 - The Ankara bombing kills at least 37 people.
  • Year: 2013 - The 2013 papal conclave elects Cardinal Jorge Mario Bergoglio taking the name Pope Francis as the 266th Pope of the Catholic Church.
  • Year: 2012 - The Sierre coach crash kills 28 people, including 22 children.
  • Year: 2003 - An article in Nature identifies the Ciampate del Diavolo as 350,000-year-old hominid footprints.
  • Year: 1997 - The Missionaries of Charity choose Sister Nirmala to succeed Mother Teresa as their leader.
  • Year: 1996 - The Dunblane massacre leads to the death of sixteen primary school children and one teacher in Dunblane, Scotland.
  • Year: 1993 - The 1993 Storm of the Century affects the eastern United States, dropping feet of snow in many areas.
  • Year: 1992 - The Mw  6.6 Erzincan earthquake strikes eastern Turkey with a maximum Mercalli intensity of VIII (Severe).
  • Year: 1988 - The Seikan Tunnel, the longest tunnel in the world with an undersea segment, opens between Aomori and Hakodate, Japan.
  • Year: 1979 - The New Jewel Movement, headed by Maurice Bishop, ousts the Prime Minister of Grenada, Eric Gairy, in a coup d'état.
  • Year: 1974 - Sierra Pacific Airlines Flight 802 crashes into the White Mountains near Bishop, California, killing 36.
  • Year: 1969 - Apollo 9 returns safely to Earth after testing the Lunar Module.
  • Year: 1957 - Cuban student revolutionaries storm the presidential palace in Havana in a failed attempt on the life of President Fulgencio Batista.
  • Year: 1954 - The Battle of Điện Biên Phủ begins with an artillery barrage by Viet Minh forces under Võ Nguyên Giáp; Viet Minh victory led to the end of the First Indochina War and French withdrawal from Vietnam.
  • Year: 1943 - The Holocaust: German forces liquidate the Jewish ghetto in Kraków.
  • Year: 1940 - The Winter War between Finland and the Soviet Union officially ends after the signing of the Moscow Peace Treaty.
  • Year: 1930 - The news of the discovery of Pluto is announced by Lowell Observatory.
  • Year: 1920 - The Kapp Putsch briefly ousts the Weimar Republic government from Berlin.
  • Year: 1900 - British forces occupy Bloemfontein, Orange Free State, during the Second Boer War.
  • Year: 1888 - The eruption of Ritter Island triggers tsunamis that kill up to 3,000 people on nearby islands.
  • Year: 1884 - The Siege of Khartoum begins. It lasts until January 26, 1885.
  • Year: 1862 - The Act Prohibiting the Return of Slaves is passed by the United States Congress, effectively annulling the Fugitive Slave Act of 1850 and setting the stage for the Emancipation Proclamation.
  • Year: 1848 - The German revolutions of 1848-1849 begin in Vienna.
  • Year: 1845 - Felix Mendelssohn's Violin Concerto receives its première performance in Leipzig with Ferdinand David as soloist.
  • Year: 1826 - Pope Leo XII publishes the apostolic constitution Quo Graviora in which he renewed the prohibition on Catholics joining freemasonry.
  • Year: 1811 - A French and Italian fleet is defeated by a British squadron off the island of Vis in the Adriatic during the Napoleonic Wars.
  • Year: 1809 - Gustav IV Adolf of Sweden is deposed in the Coup of 1809.
  • Year: 1781 - William Herschel discovers Uranus.
  • Year: 1741 - The Battle of Cartagena de Indias (part of the War of Jenkins' Ear) begins.
  • Year: 1697 - Nojpetén, capital of the last independent Maya kingdom, falls to Spanish conquistadors, the final step in the Spanish conquest of Guatemala.
  • Year: 1639 - Harvard College is named after clergyman John Harvard.
  • Year: 1591 - At the Battle of Tondibi in Mali, Moroccan forces of the Saadi dynasty, led by Judar Pasha, defeat the Songhai Empire, despite being outnumbered by at least five to one.
  • Year: 1567 - The Battle of Oosterweel, traditionally regarded as the start of the Eighty Years' War.
  • Year: 624 - The Battle of Badr, the first major battle between the Muslims and Quraysh.
