Historical Events for 10/04

  • Year: 2022 - Alain Aspect, John F. Clauser and Anton Zeilinger are jointly awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics.
  • Year: 2021 - Bubba Wallace becomes the first African-American Driver in the modern era of NASCAR to win a major race
  • Year: 2017 - Joint Nigerien-American Special Forces are ambushed by Islamic State militants outside the village of Tongo Tongo.
  • Year: 2010 - The Ajka plant accident in Hungary releases a million cubic metres of liquid alumina sludge, killing nine, injuring 122, and severely contaminating two major rivers.
  • Year: 2006 - WikiLeaks is launched.
  • Year: 2004 - SpaceShipOne wins the Ansari X Prize for private spaceflight.
  • Year: 2003 - The Maxim restaurant suicide bombing in Israel kills twenty-one Israelis, both Jews and Arabs.
  • Year: 2001 - Siberia Airlines Flight 1812 crashes after being struck by an errant Ukrainian missile. Seventy-eight people are killed.
  • Year: 1997 - The second largest cash robbery in U.S. history occurs in North Carolina
  • Year: 1993 - Tanks bombard the Russian parliament, while demonstrators against President Yeltsin rally outside.
  • Year: 1993 - Battle of Mogadishu occurs killing 18 U.S. Special Forces, two UN Peacekeepers and at least 600 Somalian militia men and civilians.
  • Year: 1992 - El Al Flight 1862 crashes into two apartment buildings in Amsterdam, killing 43 including 39 on the ground.
  • Year: 1992 - The Rome General Peace Accords end a 16-year civil war in Mozambique.
  • Year: 1991 - The Protocol on Environmental Protection to the Antarctic Treaty is opened for signature.
  • Year: 1985 - The Free Software Foundation is founded.
  • Year: 1983 - Richard Noble sets a new land speed record of 633.468 miles per hour (1,019.468 km/h) at the Black Rock Desert in Nevada.
  • Year: 1967 - Omar Ali Saifuddien III of Brunei abdicates in favour of his son.
  • Year: 1966 - Basutoland becomes independent from the United Kingdom and is renamed Lesotho.
  • Year: 1965 - Pope Paul VI begins the first papal visit to the Americas.
  • Year: 1963 - Hurricane Flora kills 6,000 in Cuba and Haiti.
  • Year: 1960 - An airliner crashes on takeoff from Boston's Logan International Airport, killing 62 people.
  • Year: 1958 - The current constitution of France is adopted.
  • Year: 1957 - Sputnik 1 becomes the first artificial satellite to orbit the Earth.
  • Year: 1941 - Norman Rockwell's Willie Gillis character debuts on the cover of The Saturday Evening Post.
  • Year: 1936 - The Metropolitan Police and various anti-fascist organizations violently clash in the Battle of Cable Street.
  • Year: 1927 - Gutzon Borglum begins sculpting Mount Rushmore.
  • Year: 1925 - S2, a Finnish Sokol class torpedo boat, sinks during a fierce storm near the coast of Pori in the Gulf of Bothnia, taking with it the whole crew of 53.
  • Year: 1925 - Great Syrian Revolt: Rebels led by Fawzi al-Qawuqji captured Hama from the French Mandate of Syria.
  • Year: 1920 - The Mannerheim League for Child Welfare, a Finnish non-governmental organization, is founded on the initiative of Sophie Mannerheim.
  • Year: 1918 - World War I: An explosion kills more than 100 people and destroys a Shell Loading Plant in New Jersey.
  • Year: 1917 - World War I: The Battle of Broodseinde is fought between the British and German armies in Flanders.
  • Year: 1895 - Horace Rawlins wins the first U.S. Open Men's Golf Championship.
  • Year: 1883 - First meeting of the Boys' Brigade in Glasgow, Scotland.
  • Year: 1883 - First run of the Orient Express.
  • Year: 1876 - The Agricultural and Mechanical College of Texas opens as the first public college in Texas.
  • Year: 1853 - The Crimean War begins when the Ottoman Empire declares war on the Russian Empire.
  • Year: 1830 - The Belgian Revolution takes legal form when the provisional government secedes from the Netherlands.
  • Year: 1824 - Mexico adopts a new constitution and becomes a federal republic.
  • Year: 1795 - Napoleon first rises to prominence by suppressing counter-revolutionary rioters threatening the National Convention.
  • Year: 1777 - American Revolutionary War: Troops under George Washington are repelled by British troops under William Howe.
  • Year: 1693 - Nine Years' War: Piedmontese troops are defeated by the French.
  • Year: 1636 - Thirty Years' War: The Swedish Army defeats the armies of Saxony and the Holy Roman Empire at the Battle of Wittstock.
  • Year: 1602 - Eighty Years' War and the Anglo-Spanish War: A fleet of Spanish galleys are defeated by English and Dutch galleons in the English Channel.
  • Year: 1597 - Governor Gonzalo Méndez de Canço begins to suppress a native uprising against his rule in what is now the state of Georgia.
  • Year: 1582 - The Gregorian Calendar is introduced by Pope Gregory XIII.
  • Year: 1535 - The Coverdale Bible is printed, with translations into English by William Tyndale and Myles Coverdale.
  • Year: 1511 - Formation of the Holy League of Aragon, the Papal States and Venice against France.
  • Year: 1363 - Battle of Lake Poyang: In one of the largest naval battles in history, Zhu Yuanzhang's rebels defeat rival Chen Youliang.
  • Year: 1302 - The Byzantine-Venetian War comes to an end.
  • Year: 1209 - Otto IV is crowned Emperor of the Holy Roman Empire by Pope Innocent III.
  • Year: 23 - Rebels sack the Chinese capital Chang'an during a peasant rebellion.
