Historical Events for 01/04

  • Year: 2018 - Hennenman-Kroonstad train crash: A passenger train operated by Shosholoza Meyl collides with a truck on a level crossing at Geneva Station between Hennenman and Kroonstad, Free State, South Africa. Twenty people are killed and 260 injured.
  • Year: 2013 - A gunman kills eight people in a house-to-house rampage in Kawit, Cavite, Philippines.
  • Year: 2010 - The Burj Khalifa, the current tallest building in the world, officially opens in Dubai.
  • Year: 2008 - A Let L-410 Turbolet crashes in the Los Roques Archipelago in Venezuela, killing 14 people.
  • Year: 2007 - The 110th United States Congress convenes, electing Nancy Pelosi as the first female Speaker of the House in U.S. history.
  • Year: 2006 - Ehud Olmert becomes acting Prime Minister of Israel after the incumbent, Ariel Sharon, suffers a second, apparently more serious stroke.
  • Year: 2004 - Mikheil Saakashvili is elected President of Georgia following the November 2003 Rose Revolution.
  • Year: 2004 - Spirit, a NASA Mars rover, lands successfully on Mars at 04:35 UTC.
  • Year: 2000 - A Norwegian passenger train departing from Trondheim, collides with a local train coming from Hamar in Åsta, Åmot; 19 people are killed and 68 injured in the accident.
  • Year: 1999 - Former professional wrestler Jesse Ventura is sworn in as governor of Minnesota, United States.
  • Year: 1998 - A massive ice storm hits eastern Canada and the northeastern United States, continuing through January 10 and causing widespread destruction.
  • Year: 1990 - In Pakistan's deadliest train accident an overloaded passenger train collides with an empty freight train, resulting in 307 deaths and 700 injuries.
  • Year: 1989 - Second Gulf of Sidra incident: A pair of Libyan MiG-23 "Floggers" are shot down by a pair of US Navy F-14 Tomcats during an air-to-air confrontation.
  • Year: 1987 - The Maryland train collision: An Amtrak train en route to Boston from Washington, D.C., collides with Conrail engines in Chase, Maryland, United States, killing 16 people.
  • Year: 1976 - The Troubles: The Ulster Volunteer Force shoots dead six Irish Catholic civilians in County Armagh, Northern Ireland. The next day, gunmen would shoot dead ten Protestant civilians nearby in retaliation.
  • Year: 1975 - This date overflowed the 12-bit field that had been used in TOPS-10. There were numerous problems and crashes related to this bug while an alternative format was developed.
  • Year: 1972 - Rose Heilbron becomes the first female judge to sit at the Old Bailey in London, UK.
  • Year: 1959 - Luna 1 becomes the first spacecraft to reach the vicinity of the Moon.
  • Year: 1958 - Sputnik 1, the first artificial Earth satellite, launched by the Soviet Union in 1957, falls to Earth from orbit.
  • Year: 1956 - The Greek National Radical Union is formed by Konstantinos Karamanlis.
  • Year: 1951 - Korean War: Chinese and North Korean forces capture Seoul for the second time.
  • Year: 1948 - Burma gains its independence from the United Kingdom, becoming an independent republic.
  • Year: 1946 - The first day of a three-day “disastrous” tornado outbreak across the south-central United States leaves 41 people dead and at least 412 others injured.
  • Year: 1944 - World War II: Operation Carpetbagger, involving the dropping of arms and supplies to resistance fighters in Europe, begins.
  • Year: 1918 - The Finnish Declaration of Independence is recognized by Russia, Sweden, Germany and France.
  • Year: 1912 - The Scout Association is incorporated throughout the British Empire by royal charter.
  • Year: 1909 - Explorer Aeneas Mackintosh of the Imperial Trans-Antarctic Expedition escaped death by fleeing across ice floes.
  • Year: 1903 - Topsy, an elephant, is electrocuted by the owners of Luna Park, Coney Island. The Edison film company records the film Electrocuting an Elephant of Topsy's death.
  • Year: 1896 - Utah is admitted as the 45th U.S. state.
  • Year: 1885 - Sino-French War: French troops under General Oscar de Négrier defeat a numerically superior Qing force at Núi Bop in northern Vietnam.
  • Year: 1884 - The Fabian Society is founded in London, United Kingdom.
  • Year: 1878 - Russo-Turkish War (1877-78): Sofia is liberated from Ottoman rule.
  • Year: 1863 - The New Apostolic Church, a Christian and chiliastic church, is established in Hamburg, Germany.
  • Year: 1854 - The McDonald Islands are discovered by Captain William McDonald aboard the Samarang.
  • Year: 1853 - After having been kidnapped and sold into slavery in the American South, Solomon Northup regains his freedom; his memoir Twelve Years a Slave later becomes a national bestseller.
  • Year: 1798 - Constantine Hangerli arrives in Bucharest, Wallachia, as its new Prince, invested by the Ottoman Empire.
  • Year: 1762 - Great Britain declares war on Spain, which meant the entry of Spain into the Seven Years' War.
  • Year: 1717 - The Netherlands, Great Britain, and France sign the Triple Alliance.
  • Year: 1649 - English Civil War: The Rump Parliament votes to put Charles I on trial.
  • Year: 1642 - English Civil War: King Charles I, accompanied by 400 soldiers, attempts to arrest five members of Parliament for treason, only to discover the men had been tipped off and fled.
  • Year: 871 - Battle of Reading: Æthelred of Wessex and his brother Alfred are defeated by a Danish invasion army.
  • Year: 46 BC - Julius Caesar fights Titus Labienus in the Battle of Ruspina.
