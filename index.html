Historical Events for 02/09

  • Year: 2021 - Second impeachment trial of Donald Trump begins.
  • Year: 2020 - Salvadoran President Nayib Bukele has the army soldiers enter the Legislative Assembly to assist in pushing for the approval for a better government security plan, causing a brief political crisis.
  • Year: 2018 - Winter Olympics: Opening ceremony is performed in Pyeongchang County in South Korea.

  • Year: 1971 - Satchel Paige becomes the first Negro league player to be voted into the USA's Baseball Hall of Fame.
  • Year: 1971 - The 6.5-6.7 Mw  Sylmar earthquake hits the Greater Los Angeles Area with a maximum Mercalli intensity of XI (Extreme), killing 64 and injuring 2,000.
  • Year: 1965 - Vietnam War: The United States Marine Corps sends a MIM-23 Hawk missile battalion to South Vietnam, the first American troops in-country without an official advisory or training mission.

  • Year: 1950 - Second Red Scare: US Senator Joseph McCarthy accuses the United States Department of State of being filled with Communists.
  • Year: 1945 - World War II: A force of Allied aircraft unsuccessfully attack a German destroyer in Førdefjorden, Norway.
  • Year: 1945 - World War II: Battle of the Atlantic: HMS Venturer sinks U-864 off the coast of Fedje, Norway, in a rare instance of submarine-to-submarine combat.
  • Year: 1943 - World War II: Pacific War: Allied authorities declare Guadalcanal secure after Imperial Japan evacuates its remaining forces from the island, ending the Battle of Guadalcanal.
  • Year: 1942 - Year-round Daylight saving time (aka War Time) is reinstated in the United States as a wartime measure to help conserve energy resources.
  • Year: 1941 - World War II: Bombing of Genoa: The Cathedral of San Lorenzo in Genoa, Italy, is struck by a bomb which fails to detonate.
  • Year: 1934 - The Balkan Entente is formed between Greece, Romania, Yugoslavia, and Turkey.
  • Year: 1932 - Prohibition law is abolished in Finland after a national referendum, where 70% voted for a repeal of the law.
  • Year: 1929 - Members of the Việt Nam Quốc Dân Đảng assassinate the labor recruiter Bazin, prompting a crackdown by French colonial authorities.

  • Year: 1913 - A group of meteors is visible across much of the eastern seaboard of the Americas, leading astronomers to conclude the source had been a small, short-lived natural satellite of the Earth.
  • Year: 1907 - The Mud March is the first large procession organised by the National Union of Women's Suffrage Societies (NUWSS).
  • Year: 1904 - Russo-Japanese War: Battle of Port Arthur concludes.
  • Year: 1900 - The Davis Cup competition is established.
  • Year: 1895 - William G. Morgan creates a game called Mintonette, which soon comes to be referred to as volleyball.

  • Year: 1870 - US president Ulysses S. Grant signs a joint resolution of Congress establishing the U.S. Weather Bureau.

  • Year: 1849 - The new Roman Republic is declared.
