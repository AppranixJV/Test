Historical Events for 10/03

  • Year: 2022 - Svante Pääbo is awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine.
  • Year: 2021 - Eight people are killed in an airplane crash near Milan, Italy.
  • Year: 2015 - Forty-two people are killed and 33 go missing in the Kunduz hospital airstrike in Afghanistan.
  • Year: 2013 - At least 360 migrants are killed when their boat sinks near the Italian island of Lampedusa.
  • Year: 2009 - Azerbaijan, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and Turkey join in the Turkic Council.
  • Year: 2008 - The Emergency Economic Stabilization Act of 2008 for the U.S. financial system is signed by President George W. Bush.
  • Year: 1995 - O. J. Simpson murder case: O. J. Simpson is acquitted of the murders of Nicole Brown Simpson and Ronald Goldman.
  • Year: 1993 - An American attack against a warlord in Mogadishu fails; eighteen US soldiers and over 350 Somalis die.
  • Year: 1991 - Nadine Gordimer is announced as the winner of the Nobel Prize in Literature.
  • Year: 1990 - The German Democratic Republic is abolished and becomes part of the Federal Republic of Germany; the event is afterwards celebrated as German Unity Day.
  • Year: 1989 - A coup in Panama City is suppressed and 11 participants are executed.
  • Year: 1986 - TASCC, a superconducting cyclotron at the Chalk River Laboratories in Canada, is officially opened.
  • Year: 1985 - The Space Shuttle Atlantis makes its maiden flight, carrying two DSCS-III Satellites on STS-51-J.
  • Year: 1981 - The hunger strike at the Maze Prison in Northern Ireland ends after seven months and ten deaths.
  • Year: 1963 - A violent coup in Honduras begins two decades of military rule.
  • Year: 1962 - Project Mercury: US astronaut Wally Schirra, in Sigma 7, is launched from Cape Canaveral for a six-orbit flight.
  • Year: 1957 - The California State Superior Court rules that the book Howl and Other Poems is not obscene.
  • Year: 1952 - The United Kingdom successfully tests a nuclear weapon in the Montebello Islands, Western Australia, to become the world's third nuclear power.
  • Year: 1951 - Korean War: The First Battle of Maryang San pits Commonwealth troops against communist Chinese troops.
  • Year: 1949 - WERD, the first black-owned radio station in the United States, opens in Atlanta.
  • Year: 1946 - An American Overseas Airlines Douglas DC-4 crashes near Ernest Harmon Air Force Base in Stephenville, Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada, killing 39.
  • Year: 1943 - World War II: German forces murder 92 civilians in Lingiades, Greece.
  • Year: 1942 - A German V-2 rocket reaches a record 85 km (46 nm) in altitude.
  • Year: 1935 - Second Italo-Abyssinian War: Italy invades Ethiopia.
  • Year: 1932 - The Kingdom of Iraq gains independence from the United Kingdom.
  • Year: 1929 - The Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes is renamed to Yugoslavia by King Alexander I.
  • Year: 1919 - Cincinnati Reds pitcher Adolfo Luque becomes the first Latin American player to appear in a World Series.
  • Year: 1918 - Tsar Boris III of Bulgaria accedes to the throne.
  • Year: 1912 - U.S. forces defeat Nicaraguan rebels at the Battle of Coyotepe Hill.
  • Year: 1873 - Chief Kintpuash and companions are hanged for their part in the Modoc War of northern California.
  • Year: 1863 - The last Thursday in November is declared as Thanksgiving Day by U.S. President Abraham Lincoln.
  • Year: 1792 - A militia departs from the Spanish stronghold of Valdivia to quell a Huilliche uprising in southern Chile.
  • Year: 1789 - George Washington proclaims Thursday November 26, 1789 a Thanksgiving Day.
  • Year: 1739 - The Treaty of Niš is signed by the Ottoman Empire and Russia ending the Russian-Turkish War.
  • Year: 1712 - The Duke of Montrose issues a warrant for the arrest of Rob Roy MacGregor.
  • Year: 1683 - Qing dynasty naval commander Shi Lang receives the surrender of the Tungning kingdom on Taiwan after the Battle of Penghu.
  • Year: 1574 - The Siege of Leiden is lifted by the Watergeuzen.
  • Year: 1392 - Muhammed VII becomes the twelfth sultan of the Emirate of Granada.
  • Year: 382 - Roman Emperor Theodosius I concludes a peace treaty with the Goths and settles them in the Balkans.
  • Year: 42 BC - Liberators' civil war: Triumvirs Mark Antony and Octavian fight to a draw Caesar's assassins Brutus and Cassius in the first part of the Battle of Philippi, where Cassius commits suicide believing the battle is lost.
  • Year: 52 BC - Gallic Wars: Vercingetorix, leader of the Gauls, surrenders to the Romans under Julius Caesar, ending the siege and battle of Alesia.
  • Year: 2457 BC - Gaecheonjeol, Hwanung (환웅) purportedly descended from heaven. South Korea's National Foundation Day.
