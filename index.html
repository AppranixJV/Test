Historical Events for 02/22

  • Year: 2018 - A man throws a grenade at the U.S. embassy in Podgorica, Montenegro. He dies at the scene from a second explosion, with no one else hurt.
  • Year: 2015 - A ferry carrying 100 passengers capsizes in the Padma River, killing 70 people.
  • Year: 2014 - President Viktor Yanukovych of Ukraine is impeached by the Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine by a vote of 328-0, fulfilling a major goal of the Euromaidan rebellion.
  • Year: 2012 - A train crash in Buenos Aires, Argentina, kills 51 people and injures 700 others.
  • Year: 2011 - Bahraini uprising: Tens of thousands of people march in protest against the deaths of seven victims killed by police and army forces during previous protests.
  • Year: 2011 - New Zealand's second deadliest earthquake strikes Christchurch, killing 185 people.
  • Year: 2006 - At least six men stage Britain's biggest robbery, stealing £53m (about $92.5 million or €78 million) from a Securitas depot in Tonbridge, Kent.
  • Year: 2006 - At approximately 6:44 a.m. local Iraqi time, explosions occurred at the al-Askari Shrine in Samarra, Iraq. The attack on the shrine, one of the holiest sites in Shia Islam, caused the escalation of sectarian tensions in Iraq into a full-scale civil war.
  • Year: 2005 - The 6.4 Mw  Zarand earthquake shakes the Kerman Province of Iran with a maximum Mercalli intensity of VIII (Severe), leaving 612 people dead and 1,411 injured.
  • Year: 2002 - Angolan political and rebel leader Jonas Savimbi is killed in a military ambush.
  • Year: 1997 - In Roslin, Midlothian, British scientists announce that an adult sheep named Dolly has been successfully cloned.
  • Year: 1995 - The Corona reconnaissance satellite program, in existence from 1959 to 1972, is declassified.
  • Year: 1994 - Aldrich Ames and his wife are charged by the United States Department of Justice with spying for the Soviet Union.
  • Year: 1986 - Start of the People Power Revolution in the Philippines.
  • Year: 1983 - The notorious Broadway flop Moose Murders opens and closes on the same night at the Eugene O'Neill Theatre.
  • Year: 1980 - Miracle on Ice: In Lake Placid, New York, the United States hockey team defeats the Soviet Union hockey team 4-3.
  • Year: 1979 - Saint Lucia gains independence from the United Kingdom.
  • Year: 1974 - Samuel Byck attempts to hijack an aircraft at Baltimore/Washington International Airport with the intention of crashing it into the White House to assassinate Richard Nixon, but is killed by police.
  • Year: 1974 - The Organisation of the Islamic Conference summit begins in Lahore, Pakistan. Thirty-seven countries attend and twenty-two heads of state and government participate. It also recognizes Bangladesh.
  • Year: 1973 - Cold War: Following President Richard Nixon's visit to the People's Republic of China, the two countries agree to establish liaison offices.
  • Year: 1972 - The Official Irish Republican Army detonates a car bomb at Aldershot barracks, killing seven and injuring nineteen others.
  • Year: 1959 - Lee Petty wins the first Daytona 500.
  • Year: 1958 - Following a plebiscite in both countries the previous day, Egypt and Syria join to form the United Arab Republic.
  • Year: 1957 - Ngô Đình Diệm of South Vietnam survives a communist shooting assassination attempt in Buôn Ma Thuột.
  • Year: 1946 - The "Long Telegram", proposing how the United States should deal with the Soviet Union, arrives from the US embassy in Moscow.
  • Year: 1944 - World War II: The Soviet Red Army recaptures Krivoi Rog.
  • Year: 1944 - World War II: American aircraft mistakenly bomb the Dutch towns of Nijmegen, Arnhem, Enschede and Deventer, resulting in 800 dead in Nijmegen alone.
  • Year: 1943 - World War II: Members of the White Rose resistance, Sophie Scholl, Hans Scholl, and Christoph Probst are executed in Nazi Germany.
  • Year: 1942 - World War II: President Franklin D. Roosevelt orders General Douglas MacArthur out of the Philippines as the Japanese victory becomes inevitable.
  • Year: 1921 - After Russian forces under Baron Roman von Ungern-Sternberg drive the Chinese out, the Bogd Khan is reinstalled as the emperor of Mongolia.[citation needed]
  • Year: 1909 - The sixteen battleships of the Great White Fleet, led by USS Connecticut, return to the United States after a voyage around the world.
  • Year: 1904 - The United Kingdom sells a meteorological station on the South Orkney Islands to Argentina; the islands are subsequently claimed by the United Kingdom in 1908.
  • Year: 1899 - Filipino forces led by General Antonio Luna launch counterattacks for the first time against the American forces during the Philippine-American War. The Filipinos fail to regain Manila from the Americans.
  • Year: 1889 - President Grover Cleveland signs a bill admitting North Dakota, South Dakota, Montana and Washington as U.S. states.
  • Year: 1881 - Cleopatra's Needle, a 3,500-year-old Ancient Egyptian obelisk is erected in Central Park, New York.
  • Year: 1879 - In Utica, New York, Frank Woolworth opens the first of many of five-and-dime Woolworth stores.
  • Year: 1872 - The Prohibition Party holds its first national convention in Columbus, Ohio, nominating James Black as its presidential nominee.
  • Year: 1862 - American Civil War: Jefferson Davis is officially inaugurated for a six-year term as the President of the Confederate States of America in Richmond, Virginia. He was previously inaugurated as a provisional president on February 18, 1861.
  • Year: 1856 - The United States Republican Party opens its first national convention in Pittsburgh.
  • Year: 1848 - The French Revolution of 1848, which would lead to the establishment of the French Second Republic, begins.
  • Year: 1847 - Mexican-American War: The Battle of Buena Vista: Five thousand American troops defeat 15,000 Mexican troops.
  • Year: 1819 - By the Adams-Onís Treaty, Spain sells Florida to the United States for five million U.S. dollars.
  • Year: 1797 - The last Invasion of Britain begins near Fishguard, Wales.
  • Year: 1770 - British customs officer Ebenezer Richardson fires blindly into a crowd during a protest in North End, Boston, fatally wounding 11-year-old Christopher Seider; the first American fatality of the American Revolution.
  • Year: 1744 - War of the Austrian Succession: The Battle of Toulon causes several Royal Navy captains to be court-martialed, and the Articles of War to be amended.
  • Year: 1651 - St. Peter's Flood: A storm surge floods the Frisian coast, drowning 15,000 people.
  • Year: 1632 - Ferdinando II de' Medici, Grand Duke of Tuscany, the dedicatee, receives the first printed copy of Galileo's Dialogue Concerning the Two Chief World Systems.
  • Year: 1495 - King Charles VIII of France enters Naples to claim the city's throne.
  • Year: 1371 - Robert II becomes King of Scotland, beginning the Stuart dynasty.
  • Year: 1316 - The Battle of Picotin, between Ferdinand of Majorca and the forces of Matilda of Hainaut, ends in victory for Ferdinand.
  • Year: 1076 - Having received a letter during the Lenten synod of 14-20 February demanding that he abdicate, Pope Gregory VII excommunicates Henry IV, Holy Roman Emperor.
