Historical Events for 04/10

  • Year: 2023 - A mass shooting occurs at the Old National Bank in Louisville, Kentucky that leaves five victims dead and eight wounded.
  • Year: 2019 - Scientists from the Event Horizon Telescope project announce the first ever image of a black hole, which was located in the centre of the M87 galaxy.
  • Year: 2016 - An earthquake of 6.6 magnitude strikes 39 km west-southwest of Ashkasham, impacting India, Afghanistan, Tajikistan, Srinagar and Pakistan.
  • Year: 2016 - The Paravur temple accident in which a devastating fire caused by the explosion of firecrackers stored for Vishu, kills more than one hundred people out of the thousands gathered for seventh day of Bhadrakali worship.
  • Year: 2010 - Polish Air Force Tu-154M crashes near Smolensk, Russia, killing 96 people, including Polish President Lech Kaczyński, his wife, and dozens of other senior officials and dignitaries.
  • Year: 2009 - President of Fiji Ratu Josefa Iloilo announces the abrogation of the constitution and assumes all governance in the country, creating a constitutional crisis.
  • Year: 1998 - The Good Friday Agreement is signed in Northern Ireland.
  • Year: 1991 - A rare tropical storm develops in the South Atlantic Ocean near Angola; the first to be documented by satellites.
  • Year: 1991 - Italian ferry MS Moby Prince collides with an oil tanker in dense fog off Livorno, Italy, killing 140.
  • Year: 1988 - The Ojhri Camp explosion kills or injures more than 1,000 people in Rawalpindi and Islamabad, Pakistan.
  • Year: 1979 - Red River Valley tornado outbreak: A tornado lands in Wichita Falls, Texas killing 42 people.
  • Year: 1973 - Invicta International Airlines Flight 435 crashes in a snowstorm on approach to Basel, Switzerland, killing 108 people.
  • Year: 1972 - Vietnam War: For the first time since November 1967, American B-52 bombers reportedly begin bombing North Vietnam.
  • Year: 1972 - Tombs containing bamboo slips, among them Sun Tzu's Art of War and Sun Bin's lost military treatise, are discovered by construction workers in Shandong.
  • Year: 1971 - Ping-pong diplomacy: In an attempt to thaw relations with the United States, China hosts the U.S. table tennis team for a week-long visit.
  • Year: 1970 - Paul McCartney announces that he is leaving The Beatles for personal and professional reasons.
  • Year: 1968 - The TEV Wahine, a New Zealand ferry sinks in Wellington harbour due to a fierce storm - the strongest winds ever in Wellington. Out of the 734 people on board, fifty-three died.
  • Year: 1963 - One hundred twenty-nine American sailors die when the submarine USS Thresher sinks at sea.
  • Year: 1944 - Rudolf Vrba and Alfréd Wetzler escape from Birkenau death camp.
  • Year: 1941 - World War II: The Axis powers establish the Independent State of Croatia.
  • Year: 1939 - Alcoholics Anonymous, A.A.'s "Big Book", is first published.
  • Year: 1938 - The 1938 German parliamentary election and referendum seeks approval for a single list of Nazi candidates and the recent annexation of Austria.
  • Year: 1925 - The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald is first published in New York City, by Charles Scribner's Sons.
  • Year: 1919 - The Third Regional Congress of Peasants, Workers and Insurgents is held by the Makhnovshchina at Huliaipole.
  • Year: 1919 - Mexican Revolution leader Emiliano Zapata is ambushed and shot dead by government forces in Morelos.
  • Year: 1916 - The Professional Golfers' Association of America (PGA) is created in New York City.
  • Year: 1912 - RMS Titanic sets sail from Southampton, England on her maiden and only voyage.
  • Year: 1900 - British suffer a sharp defeat by the Boers south of Brandfort. 600 British troops are killed and wounded and 800 taken prisoner.
  • Year: 1896 - 1896 Summer Olympics: The Olympic marathon is run ending with the victory of Greek athlete Spyridon Louis.
  • Year: 1887 - On Easter Sunday, Pope Leo XIII authorizes the establishment of the Catholic University of America.
  • Year: 1875 - India: Arya Samaj is founded in Mumbai by Swami Dayananda Saraswati to propagate his goal of social reform.
  • Year: 1872 - The first Arbor Day is celebrated in Nebraska.
  • Year: 1868 - At Arogee in Abyssinia, British and Indian forces defeat an army of Emperor Tewodros II. While 700 Ethiopians are killed and many more injured, only two British/Indian troops die.
  • Year: 1866 - The American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (ASPCA) is founded in New York City by Henry Bergh.
  • Year: 1865 - American Civil War: A day after his surrender to Union forces, Confederate General Robert E. Lee addresses his troops for the last time.
  • Year: 1864 - Archduke Maximilian of Habsburg is proclaimed emperor of Mexico during the French intervention in Mexico.
  • Year: 1858 - After the original Big Ben, a 14.5 tonnes (32,000 lb) bell for the Palace of Westminster, had cracked during testing, it is recast into the current 13.76 tonnes (30,300 lb) bell by Whitechapel Bell Foundry.
  • Year: 1826 - The 10,500 inhabitants of the Greek town of Missolonghi begin leaving the town after a year's siege by Turkish forces. Very few of them survive.
  • Year: 1821 - Greek War of Independence: the island of Psara joins the Greek struggle for independence.
  • Year: 1821 - Patriarch Gregory V of Constantinople is hanged by the Ottoman government from the main gate of the Patriarchate and his body is thrown into the Bosphorus.
  • Year: 1816 - The Federal government of the United States approves the creation of the Second Bank of the United States.
  • Year: 1815 - The Mount Tambora volcano begins a three-month-long eruption, lasting until July 15. The eruption ultimately kills 71,000 people and affects Earth's climate for the next two years.
  • Year: 1809 - Napoleonic Wars: The War of the Fifth Coalition begins when forces of the Austrian Empire invade Bavaria.
  • Year: 1741 - War of the Austrian Succession: Prussia gains control of Silesia at the Battle of Mollwitz.
  • Year: 1717 - Robert Walpole resigns from the British government, commencing the Whig Split which lasts until 1720.
  • Year: 1710 - The Statute of Anne, the first law regulating copyright, comes into force in Great Britain.
  • Year: 1606 - The Virginia Company of London is established by royal charter by James I of England with the purpose of establishing colonial settlements in North America.
  • Year: 1545 - The settlement of Villa Imperial de Carlos V (now the city of Potosí) in Bolivia is founded after the discovery of huge silver deposits in the area.
  • Year: 1500 - Ludovico Sforza is captured by Swiss troops at Novara and is handed over to the French.
  • Year: 1407 - Deshin Shekpa, 5th Karmapa Lama visits the Ming dynasty capital at Nanjing and is awarded the title "Great Treasure Prince of Dharma".
  • Year: 837 - Halley's Comet makes its closest approach to Earth at a distance equal to 0.0342 AU (5.1 million kilometres/3.2 million miles).
  • Year: 428 - Nestorius becomes the Patriarch of Constantinople.
