Historical Events for 03/22

  • Year: 2021 - Ten people are killed in a mass shooting in Boulder, Colorado.
  • Year: 2020 - Greek Prime Minister Kyriakos Mitsotakis announces a national lockdown and the country's first ever self-imposed curfew, in an effort to fight the spread of COVID-19.
  • Year: 2020 - Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi announces the country's largest ever self-imposed curfew, in an effort to fight the spread of COVID-19.
  • Year: 2019 - Two buses crashed in Kitampo, a town north of Ghana's capital Accra, killing at least 50 people.
  • Year: 2019 - The Special Counsel investigation on the 2016 United States presidential election concludes when Robert Mueller submits his report to the United States Attorney General.
  • Year: 2017 - Syrian civil war: Five hundred members of the Syrian Democratic Forces (SDF) are airlifted south of the Euphrates by United States Air Force helicopters, beginning the Battle of Tabqa.
  • Year: 2017 - A terrorist attack in London near the Houses of Parliament leaves four people dead and at least 20 injured.
  • Year: 2016 - Three suicide bombers kill 32 people and injure 316 in the 2016 Brussels bombings at the airport and at the Maelbeek/Maalbeek metro station.
  • Year: 2013 - At least 37 people are killed and 200 are injured after a fire destroys a camp containing Burmese refugees near Ban Mae, Thailand.
  • Year: 2006 - Three Christian Peacemaker Team (CPT) hostages are freed by British forces in Baghdad after 118 days of captivity and the murder of their colleague from the U.S., Tom Fox.
  • Year: 2004 - Ahmed Yassin, co-founder and leader of the Palestinian Sunni Islamist group Hamas, two bodyguards, and nine civilian bystanders are killed in the Gaza Strip when hit by Israeli Air Force Hellfire missiles.
  • Year: 1997 - Comet Hale-Bopp reaches its closest approach to Earth at 1.315 AU.
  • Year: 1997 - Tara Lipinski, aged 14 years and nine months, becomes the youngest women's World Figure Skating Champion.
  • Year: 1995 - Cosmonaut Valeri Polyakov returns to earth after setting a record of 438 days in space.
  • Year: 1993 - The Intel Corporation ships the first Pentium chips (80586), featuring a 60 MHz clock speed, 100+ MIPS, and a 64 bit data path.
  • Year: 1992 - Fall of communism in Albania: The Democratic Party of Albania wins a decisive majority in the parliamentary election.
  • Year: 1992 - USAir Flight 405 crashes shortly after takeoff from New York City's LaGuardia Airport, leading to a number of studies into the effect that ice has on aircraft.
  • Year: 1988 - The United States Congress votes to override President Ronald Reagan's veto of the Civil Rights Restoration Act of 1987.
  • Year: 1982 - NASA's Space Shuttle Columbia is launched from the Kennedy Space Center on its third mission, STS-3.
  • Year: 1978 - Karl Wallenda of The Flying Wallendas dies after falling off a tight-rope suspended between two hotels in San Juan, Puerto Rico.
  • Year: 1975 - A fire at the Browns Ferry Nuclear Power Plant in Decatur, Alabama causes a dangerous reduction in cooling water levels.
  • Year: 1972 - In Eisenstadt v. Baird, the United States Supreme Court decides that unmarried persons have the right to possess contraceptives.
  • Year: 1972 - The United States Congress sends the Equal Rights Amendment to the states for ratification.
  • Year: 1970 - Chicano residents in San Diego, California occupy a site under the Coronado Bridge, leading to the creation of Chicano Park.
  • Year: 1963 - The Beatles release their debut album Please Please Me.
  • Year: 1960 - Arthur Leonard Schawlow and Charles Hard Townes receive the first patent for a laser.
  • Year: 1946 - The United Kingdom grants full independence to Transjordan.
  • Year: 1945 - The Arab League is founded when a charter is adopted in Cairo, Egypt.
  • Year: 1945 - World War II: The city of Hildesheim, Germany is heavily damaged in a British air raid, though it had little military significance and Germany was on the verge of final defeat.
  • Year: 1943 - World War II: The entire village of Khatyn (in what is the present-day Republic of Belarus) is burnt alive by Schutzmannschaft Battalion 118.
  • Year: 1942 - World War II: In the Mediterranean Sea, the Royal Navy confronts Italy's Regia Marina in the Second Battle of Sirte.
  • Year: 1939 - Germany takes Memel from Lithuania.
  • Year: 1934 - The first Masters Tournament is held at Augusta National Golf Club in Georgia.
  • Year: 1933 - Nazi Germany opens its first concentration camp, Dachau.
  • Year: 1933 - Cullen-Harrison Act: President Franklin D. Roosevelt signs an amendment to the Volstead Act, legalizing the manufacture and sale of "3.2 beer" (3.2% alcohol by weight, approximately 4% alcohol by volume) and light wines.
  • Year: 1920 - Azeri and Turkish army soldiers with participation of Kurdish gangs attack the Armenian inhabitants of Shushi (Nagorno Karabakh).
  • Year: 1916 - Yuan Shikai abdicates as Emperor of China, restoring the Republic and returning to the Presidency.
  • Year: 1913 - Mystic Phan Xích Long, the self-proclaimed Emperor of Vietnam, is arrested for organising a revolt against the colonial rule of French Indochina, which was nevertheless carried out by his supporters the following day.
  • Year: 1906 - The first England vs France rugby union match is played at Parc des Princes in Paris.
  • Year: 1896 - Charilaos Vasilakos wins the first modern Olympic marathon race with a time of three hours and 18 minutes.
  • Year: 1895 - Before the Société pour L'Encouragement à l'Industrie, brothers Auguste and Louis Lumière demonstrate movie film technology publicly for the first time.
  • Year: 1894 - The Stanley Cup ice hockey competition is held for the first time, in Montreal, Canada.
  • Year: 1873 - The Spanish National Assembly abolishes slavery in Puerto Rico.
  • Year: 1871 - In North Carolina, William Woods Holden becomes the first governor of a U.S. state to be removed from office by impeachment.
  • Year: 1849 - The Austrians defeat the Piedmontese at the Battle of Novara.
  • Year: 1829 - In the London Protocol, the three protecting powers (United Kingdom, France and Russia) establish the borders of Greece.
  • Year: 1794 - The Slave Trade Act of 1794 bans the export of slaves from the United States, and prohibits American citizens from outfitting a ship for the purpose of importing slaves.
  • Year: 1792 - Battle of Croix-des-Bouquets: Black slave insurgents gain a victory in the first major battle of the Haitian Revolution.
  • Year: 1784 - The Emerald Buddha is moved with great ceremony to its current location in Wat Phra Kaew, Thailand.
  • Year: 1765 - The British Parliament passes the Stamp Act that introduces a tax to be levied directly on its American colonies.
  • Year: 1739 - Nader Shah occupies Delhi in India and sacks the city, stealing the jewels of the Peacock Throne.
  • Year: 1638 - Anne Hutchinson is expelled from Massachusetts Bay Colony for religious dissent.
  • Year: 1631 - The Massachusetts Bay Colony outlaws the possession of cards, dice, and gaming tables.
  • Year: 1622 - Jamestown massacre: Algonquians kill 347 English settlers around Jamestown, Virginia, a third of the colony's population, during the Second Anglo-Powhatan War.
  • Year: 1621 - The Pilgrims of Plymouth Colony sign a peace treaty with Massasoit of the Wampanoags.
  • Year: 1508 - Ferdinand II of Aragon commissions Amerigo Vespucci chief navigator of the Spanish Empire.
  • Year: 1312 - Vox in excelso: Pope Clement V dissolves the Order of the Knights Templar.
  • Year: 1185 - Battle of Yashima: the Japanese forces of the Taira clan are defeated by the Minamoto clan.
  • Year: 871 - Æthelred of Wessex is defeated by a Danish invasion army at the Battle of Marton.
  • Year: 235 - Roman emperor Severus Alexander is murdered, marking the start of the Crisis of the Third Century.
  • Year: 106 - Start of the Bostran era, the calendar of the province of Arabia Petraea.
