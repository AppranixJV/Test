Historical Events for 12/29

  • Year: 2020 - A magnitude 6.4 earthquake hits near the town of Petrinja in Sisak-Moslavina County, Croatia, killing 7 people.
  • Year: 2020 - A large explosion at the airport in the southern Yemeni city of Aden kills at least 22 people and wounds 50.
  • Year: 2013 - Seven-time Formula One champion Michael Schumacher suffers a massive head injury while skiing in the French Alps.
  • Year: 2013 - A suicide bomb attack at the Volgograd-1 railway station in the southern Russian city of Volgograd kills at least 18 people and wounds 40 others.
  • Year: 2012 - A Tupolev Tu-204 airliner crashes in a ditch between the airport fence and the M3 highway after overshooting a runway at Vnukovo International Airport in Moscow, Russia, killing five people and leaving three others critically injured.
  • Year: 2006 - The UK settles its Anglo-American loan, post-WWII loan debt.
  • Year: 2003 - The last known speaker of Akkala Sami dies, rendering the language extinct.
  • Year: 1998 - Leaders of the Khmer Rouge apologize for the Cambodian genocide that claimed over one million lives.
  • Year: 1996 - Guatemala and leaders of Guatemalan National Revolutionary Unity sign a peace accord ending a 36-year civil war.
  • Year: 1994 - Turkish Airlines Flight 278 (a Boeing 737-400) crashes on approach to Van Ferit Melen Airport in Van, Turkey, killing 57 of the 76 people on board.
  • Year: 1992 - Fernando Collor de Mello, president of Brazil, tries to resign amidst corruption charges, but is then impeached.
  • Year: 1989 - The Nikkei 225 for the Tokyo Stock Exchange hits its all-time intra-day high of 38,957.44 and closing high at 38,915.87, serving as the apex of the Japanese asset price bubble.
  • Year: 1989 - Czech writer, philosopher and dissident Václav Havel is elected the first post-communist President of Czechoslovakia.
  • Year: 1975 - A bomb explodes at LaGuardia Airport in New York City, killing 11 people and injuring more than 75.
  • Year: 1972 - Eastern Air Lines Flight 401 (a Lockheed L-1011 TriStar) crashes in the Florida Everglades on approach to Miami International Airport, Florida, killing 101 of the 176 people on board.
  • Year: 1940 - In the Second Great Fire of London, the Luftwaffe fire-bombs London, England, killing almost 200 civilians during World War II.
  • Year: 1937 - The Irish Free State is replaced by a new state called Ireland with the adoption of a new constitution.
  • Year: 1934 - Japan renounces the Washington Naval Treaty of 1922 and the London Naval Treaty of 1930.
  • Year: 1930 - Sir Muhammad Iqbal's presidential address in Allahabad introduces the two-nation theory and outlines a vision for the creation of Pakistan.
  • Year: 1913 - Cecil B. DeMille starts filming Hollywood's first feature film, The Squaw Man.
  • Year: 1911 - Mongolia gains independence from the Qing dynasty, enthroning 8th Jebtsundamba Khutughtu as Khagan of Mongolia.
  • Year: 1890 - On Pine Ridge Indian Reservation, 300 Lakota are killed by the United States 7th Cavalry Regiment.
  • Year: 1876 - The Ashtabula River railroad disaster occurs, leaving 64 injured and 92 dead at Ashtabula, Ohio.
  • Year: 1874 - The military coup of Gen. Martinez Campos in Sagunto ends the failed First Spanish Republic and the monarchy is restored as Prince Alfonso is proclaimed King of Spain.
  • Year: 1860 - The launch of HMS Warrior, with her combination of screw propeller, iron hull and iron armour, renders all previous warships obsolete.
  • Year: 1845 - The United States annexes the Republic of Texas and admits it as the 28th state.
  • Year: 1835 - The Treaty of New Echota is signed, ceding all the lands of the Cherokee east of the Mississippi River to the United States.
  • Year: 1812 - USS Constitution, under the command of Captain William Bainbridge, captures HMS Java off the coast of Brazil after a three-hour battle.
  • Year: 1778 - American Revolutionary War: Three thousand British soldiers under the command of Lieutenant Colonel Archibald Campbell capture Savannah, Georgia.
  • Year: 1607 - According to John Smith, Pocahontas, daughter of Powhatan leader Wahunsenacawh, successfully pleads for his life after tribal leaders attempt to execute him.
  • Year: 1503 - The Battle of Garigliano was fought between a Spanish army under Gonzalo Fernández de Córdoba and a French army commanded by Ludovico II, Marquess of Saluzzo.
  • Year: 1170 - Thomas Becket, Archbishop of Canterbury, is assassinated inside Canterbury Cathedral by followers of King Henry II; he subsequently becomes a saint and martyr in the Anglican Communion and the Catholic Church.
