Historical Events for 03/12

  • Year: 2020 - The United States suspends travel from Europe due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • Year: 2019 - In the House of Commons, the revised EU Withdrawal Bill was rejected by a margin of 149 votes.
  • Year: 2018 - US-Bangla Airlines Flight 211 crashes at Tribhuvan International Airport in Katmandu, killing 51 and injuring 20.
  • Year: 2014 - A gas explosion in the New York City neighborhood of East Harlem kills eight and injures 70 others.
  • Year: 2011 - A reactor at the Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant explodes and releases radioactivity into the atmosphere a day after the 2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami.
  • Year: 2009 - Financier Bernie Madoff pleads guilty to one of the largest frauds in Wall Street's history.
  • Year: 2004 - The President of South Korea, Roh Moo-hyun, is impeached by its National Assembly: the first such impeachment in the nation's history.
  • Year: 2003 - The World Health Organization officially release a global warning of outbreaks of Severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARS).
  • Year: 2003 - Zoran Đinđić, Prime Minister of Serbia, is assassinated in Belgrade.
  • Year: 1999 - Former Warsaw Pact members the Czech Republic, Hungary and Poland join NATO.
  • Year: 1993 - North Korea announces that it will withdraw from the Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons and refuses to allow inspectors access to its nuclear sites.
  • Year: 1993 - Several bombs explode in Mumbai, India, killing about 300 people and injuring hundreds more.
  • Year: 1992 - Mauritius becomes a republic while remaining a member of the Commonwealth of Nations.
  • Year: 1989 - Sir Tim Berners-Lee submits his proposal to CERN for an information management system, which subsequently develops into the World Wide Web.
  • Year: 1971 - The 1971 Turkish military memorandum is sent to the Süleyman Demirel government of Turkey and the government resigns.
  • Year: 1968 - Mauritius gains independence from the United Kingdom.
  • Year: 1967 - Suharto takes power from Sukarno when the People's Consultative Assembly inaugurate him as Acting President of Indonesia.
  • Year: 1950 - The Llandow air disaster kills 80 people when the aircraft they are travelling in crashes near Sigingstone, Wales. At the time this was the world's deadliest air disaster.
  • Year: 1947 - Cold War: The Truman Doctrine is proclaimed to help stem the spread of Communism.
  • Year: 1942 - The Battle of Java ends with the surrender of the American-British-Dutch-Australian Command to the Japanese Empire in Bandung, West Java, Dutch East Indies.
  • Year: 1940 - The most destructive train accident in Finnish history kills 39 and injures 69 people in Turenki, Janakkala.
  • Year: 1940 - Winter War: Finland signs the Moscow Peace Treaty with the Soviet Union, ceding almost all of Finnish Karelia.
  • Year: 1938 - Anschluss: German troops occupy and absorb Austria.
  • Year: 1933 - Great Depression: Franklin D. Roosevelt addresses the nation for the first time as President of the United States. This is also the first of his "fireside chats".
  • Year: 1930 - Mahatma Gandhi begins the Salt March, a 200-mile (320 km) march to the sea to protest the British monopoly on salt in India.
  • Year: 1928 - In California, the St. Francis Dam fails; the resulting floods kill 431 people.
  • Year: 1920 - The Kapp Putsch begins when the Marinebrigade Ehrhardt is ordered to march on Berlin.
  • Year: 1918 - Moscow becomes the capital of Russia again after Saint Petersburg held this status for most of the period since 1713.
  • Year: 1913 - The future capital of Australia is officially named Canberra.
  • Year: 1912 - The Girl Guides (later renamed the Girl Scouts of the USA) are founded in the United States.
  • Year: 1811 - Peninsular War: A day after a successful rearguard action, French Marshal Michel Ney once again successfully delays the pursuing Anglo-Portuguese force at the Battle of Redinha.
  • Year: 1689 - James II of England landed at Kinsale, starting the Williamite War in Ireland.
  • Year: 1622 - Ignatius of Loyola and Francis Xavier, founders of the Society of Jesus, are canonized by the Roman Catholic Church.
  • Year: 1579 - Start of the Siege of Maastricht, part of the Eighty Years' War.
  • Year: 1158 - German city Munich (München) is first mentioned as forum apud Munichen in the Augsburg arbitration by Holy Roman Emperor Friedrich I.
  • Year: 1088 - Election of Urban II as the 159th Pope of the Catholic Church. He is best known for initiating the Crusades.
  • Year: 538 - Vitiges, king of the Ostrogoths ends his siege of Rome and retreats to Ravenna, leaving the city to the victorious Byzantine general, Belisarius.
