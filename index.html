Historical Events for 12/02

  • Year: 2020 - Cannabis is removed from the list of most dangerous drugs of the international drug control treaty by the UN Commission on Narcotic Drugs.
  • Year: 2016 - Thirty-six people die in a fire at a converted Oakland, California, warehouse serving as an artist collective.
  • Year: 2015 - San Bernardino attack: Syed Rizwan Farook and Tashfeen Malik kill 14 people and wound 22 at the Inland Regional Center in San Bernardino, California.
  • Year: 2001 - Enron files for Chapter 11 bankruptcy.
  • Year: 1999 - The United Kingdom devolves political power in Northern Ireland to the Northern Ireland Executive following the Good Friday Agreement.
  • Year: 1993 - Space Shuttle program: STS-61: NASA launches the Space Shuttle Endeavour on a mission to repair the Hubble Space Telescope.
  • Year: 1993 - Colombian drug lord Pablo Escobar is shot and killed by police in Medellín.
  • Year: 1991 - Canada and Poland become the first nations to recognize the independence of Ukraine from the Soviet Union.
  • Year: 1989 - The Peace Agreement of Hat Yai is signed and ratified by the Malayan Communist Party (MCP) and the governments of Malaysia and Thailand, ending the over two-decade-long communist insurgency in Malaysia.
  • Year: 1988 - Benazir Bhutto is sworn in as Prime Minister of Pakistan, becoming the first woman to head the government of a Muslim-majority state.
  • Year: 1982 - At the University of Utah, Barney Clark becomes the first person to receive a permanent artificial heart.
  • Year: 1980 - Salvadoran Civil War: Four American missionaries are raped and murdered by a death squad.
  • Year: 1976 - Fidel Castro becomes President of Cuba, replacing Osvaldo Dorticós Torrado.
  • Year: 1975 - Laotian Civil War: The Pathet Lao seizes the Laotian capital of Vientiane, forces the abdication of King Sisavang Vatthana, and proclaims the Lao People's Democratic Republic.
  • Year: 1971 - Abu Dhabi, Ajman, Fujairah, Sharjah, Dubai, and Umm al-Quwain form the United Arab Emirates.
  • Year: 1970 - The United States Environmental Protection Agency begins operations.
  • Year: 1962 - Vietnam War: After a trip to Vietnam at the request of U.S. President John F. Kennedy, U.S. Senate Majority Leader Mike Mansfield becomes the first American official to comment adversely on the war's progress.
  • Year: 1961 - In a nationally broadcast speech, Cuban leader Fidel Castro declares that he is a Marxist-Leninist and that Cuba will adopt Communism.
  • Year: 1957 - United Nations Security Council Resolution 126 relating to the Kashmir conflict is adopted.
  • Year: 1956 - The Granma reaches the shores of Cuba's Oriente Province. Fidel Castro, Che Guevara and 80 other members of the 26th of July Movement disembark to initiate the Cuban Revolution.
  • Year: 1954 - The Sino-American Mutual Defense Treaty, between the United States and Taiwan, is signed in Washington, D.C.
  • Year: 1954 - Cold War: The United States Senate votes 65 to 22 to censure Joseph McCarthy for "conduct that tends to bring the Senate into dishonor and disrepute".
  • Year: 1950 - Korean War: The Battle of the Ch'ongch'on River ends with a decisive Chinese victory and UN forces are completely expelled from North Korea.
  • Year: 1949 - Convention for the Suppression of the Traffic in Persons and of the Exploitation of the Prostitution of Others is adopted.
  • Year: 1947 - Jerusalem Riots of 1947: Arabs riot in Jerusalem in response to the United Nations Partition Plan for Palestine.
  • Year: 1943 - World War II: A Luftwaffe bombing raid on the harbour of Bari, Italy, sinks numerous cargo and transport ships, including the American SS John Harvey, which is carrying a stockpile of mustard gas.
  • Year: 1942 - World War II: During the Manhattan Project, a team led by Enrico Fermi initiates the first artificial self-sustaining nuclear chain reaction.
  • Year: 1939 - New York City's LaGuardia Airport opens.
  • Year: 1930 - Great Depression: In a State of the Union message, U.S. President Herbert Hoover proposes a $150 million public works program to help generate jobs and stimulate the economy.
  • Year: 1927 - Following 19 years of Ford Model T production, the Ford Motor Company unveils the Ford Model A as its new automobile.
  • Year: 1917 - World War I: Russia and the Central Powers sign an armistice at Brest-Litovsk, and peace talks leading to the Treaty of Brest-Litovsk begin.
  • Year: 1908 - Puyi becomes Emperor of China at the age of two.
  • Year: 1899 - Philippine-American War: The Battle of Tirad Pass, known as the "Filipino Thermopylae", is fought.
  • Year: 1867 - At Tremont Temple in Boston, British author Charles Dickens gives his first public reading in the United States.
  • Year: 1865 - Alabama ratifies the 13th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution, followed by North Carolina, then Georgia; U.S. slaves were legally free within two weeks.
  • Year: 1859 - Militant abolitionist leader John Brown is hanged for his October 16 raid on Harpers Ferry, West Virginia.
  • Year: 1852 - Louis-Napoléon Bonaparte becomes Emperor of the French as Napoleon III.
  • Year: 1851 - French President Louis-Napoléon Bonaparte overthrows the Second Republic.
  • Year: 1848 - Franz Joseph I becomes Emperor of Austria.
  • Year: 1845 - Manifest Destiny: In a State of the Union message, U.S. President James K. Polk proposes that the United States should aggressively expand into the West.
  • Year: 1823 - Monroe Doctrine: In a State of the Union message, U.S. President James Monroe proclaims American neutrality in future European conflicts, and warns European powers not to interfere in the Americas.
  • Year: 1805 - War of the Third Coalition: Battle of Austerlitz: French troops under Napoleon decisively defeat a joint Russo-Austrian force.
  • Year: 1804 - At Notre Dame Cathedral in Paris, Napoleon Bonaparte crowns himself Emperor of the French.
  • Year: 1766 - Swedish parliament approves the Swedish Freedom of the Press Act and implements it as a ground law, thus being first in the world with freedom of speech.
  • Year: 1763 - Dedication of the Touro Synagogue, in Newport, Rhode Island, the first synagogue in what will become the United States.
  • Year: 1697 - St Paul's Cathedral, rebuilt to the design of Sir Christopher Wren following the Great Fire of London, is consecrated.
  • Year: 1409 - The University of Leipzig opens.
  • Year: 1244 - Pope Innocent IV arrives at Lyon for the First Council of Lyon.
