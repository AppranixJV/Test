Historical Events for 01/03

  • Year: 2023 - Singapore's Jurong Bird Park permanently closes.
  • Year: 2020 - Iranian General Qasem Soleimani is killed by an American airstrike near Baghdad International Airport, igniting global concerns of a potential armed conflict.
  • Year: 2019 - Chang'e 4 makes the first soft landing on the far side of the Moon, deploying the Yutu-2 lunar rover.
  • Year: 2018 - For the first time in history, all five major storm surge gates in the Netherlands are closed simultaneously in the wake of a storm.
  • Year: 2016 - In response to the execution of Nimr al-Nimr, Iran ends its diplomatic relations with Saudi Arabia.
  • Year: 2015 - Boko Haram militants destroy the entire town of Baga in north-east Nigeria, starting the Baga massacre and killing as many as 2,000 people.
  • Year: 2009 - The first block of the blockchain of the decentralized payment system Bitcoin, called the Genesis block, is established by the creator of the system, Satoshi Nakamoto.
  • Year: 2004 - Flash Airlines Flight 604 crashes into the Red Sea, resulting in 148 deaths, making it one of the deadliest aviation accidents in Egyptian history.
  • Year: 2002 - Israeli-Palestinian conflict: Israeli forces seize the Palestinian freighter Karine A in the Red Sea, finding 50 tons of weapons.
  • Year: 1999 - The Mars Polar Lander is launched by NASA.
  • Year: 1994 - Baikal Airlines Flight 130 crashes near Mamoney, Irkutsk, Russia, resulting in 125 deaths.
  • Year: 1993 - In Moscow, Russia, George H. W. Bush and Boris Yeltsin sign the second Strategic Arms Reduction Treaty (START).
  • Year: 1992 - CommutAir Flight 4821 crashes on approach to Adirondack Regional Airport, in Saranac Lake, New York, killing two people.
  • Year: 1990 - United States invasion of Panama: Manuel Noriega, former leader of Panama, surrenders to American forces.
  • Year: 1987 - Varig Flight 797 crashes near Akouré in the Ivory Coast, resulting in 50 deaths.
  • Year: 1977 - Apple Computer is incorporated.
  • Year: 1976 - The International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, adopted by the United Nations General Assembly, comes into force.
  • Year: 1962 - Pope John XXIII excommunicates Fidel Castro.
  • Year: 1961 - Aero Flight 311 crashes into the forest in Kvevlax, Finland, killing 25 people.
  • Year: 1961 - A protest by agricultural workers in Baixa de Cassanje, Portuguese Angola, turns into a revolt, opening the Angolan War of Independence, the first of the Portuguese Colonial Wars.
  • Year: 1961 - The SL-1 nuclear reactor, near Idaho Falls, Idaho, is destroyed by a steam explosion in the only reactor incident in the United States to cause immediate fatalities.
  • Year: 1961 - Cold War: After a series of economic retaliations against one another, the United States severs diplomatic relations with Cuba.
  • Year: 1959 - Alaska is admitted as the 49th U.S. state.
  • Year: 1958 - The West Indies Federation is formed.
  • Year: 1957 - The Hamilton Watch Company introduces the first electric watch.
  • Year: 1956 - A fire damages the top part of the Eiffel Tower.
  • Year: 1953 - Frances P. Bolton and her son, Oliver from Ohio, become the first mother and son to serve simultaneously in the U.S. Congress.
  • Year: 1949 - The Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas, the central bank of the Philippines, is established.
  • Year: 1947 - Proceedings of the U.S. Congress are televised for the first time.
  • Year: 1946 - Popular Canadian American jockey George Woolf dies in a freak accident during a race; the annual George Woolf Memorial Jockey Award is created to honor him.
  • Year: 1944 - World War II: US flying ace Major Greg "Pappy" Boyington is shot down in his Vought F4U Corsair by Captain Masajiro Kawato flying a Mitsubishi A6M Zero.
  • Year: 1933 - Minnie D. Craig becomes the first woman elected as Speaker of the North Dakota House of Representatives, the first woman to hold a Speaker position anywhere in the United States.
  • Year: 1920 - Over 640 are killed after a magnitude 6.4 earthquake strikes the Mexican states Puebla and Veracruz.
  • Year: 1913 - First Balkan War: Greece completes its capture of the eastern Aegean island of Chios, as the last Ottoman forces on the island surrender.
  • Year: 1913 - An Atlantic coast storm sets the lowest confirmed barometric pressure reading for a non-tropical system in the continental United States.
  • Year: 1911 - A gun battle in the East End of London leaves two dead. It sparked a political row over the involvement of then-Home Secretary Winston Churchill.
  • Year: 1911 - A magnitude 7.7 earthquake destroys the city of Almaty in Russian Turkestan.
  • Year: 1885 - Sino-French War: Beginning of the Battle of Núi Bop.
  • Year: 1871 - In the Battle of Bapaume, an engagement in the Franco-Prussian War, General Louis Faidherbe's forces bring about a Prussian retreat.
  • Year: 1870 - Construction work begins on the Brooklyn Bridge in New York, United States.
  • Year: 1868 - Meiji Restoration in Japan: The Tokugawa shogunate is abolished; agents of Satsuma and Chōshū seize power.
  • Year: 1861 - American Civil War: Delaware votes not to secede from the United States.
  • Year: 1848 - Joseph Jenkins Roberts is sworn in as the first president of Liberia.
  • Year: 1833 - Captain James Onslow, in the Clio, reasserts British sovereignty over the Falkland Islands.
  • Year: 1815 - Austria, the United Kingdom, and France form a secret defensive alliance against Prussia and Russia.
  • Year: 1777 - American General George Washington defeats British General Lord Cornwallis at the Battle of Princeton.
  • Year: 1749 - The first issue of Berlingske, Denmark's oldest continually operating newspaper, is published.
  • Year: 1749 - Benning Wentworth issues the first of the New Hampshire Grants, leading to the establishment of Vermont.
  • Year: 1653 - By the Coonan Cross Oath, the Eastern Church in India cuts itself off from colonial Portuguese tutelage.
  • Year: 1521 - Pope Leo X excommunicates Martin Luther in the papal bull Decet Romanum Pontificem.
  • Year: 250 - Emperor Decius orders everyone in the Roman Empire (except Jews) to make sacrifices to the Roman gods.
  • Year: 69 - The Roman legions on the Rhine refuse to declare their allegiance to Galba, instead proclaiming their legate, Aulus Vitellius, as emperor.
