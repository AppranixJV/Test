Historical Events for 10/10

  • Year: 2022 - Ben S. Bernanke, Douglas W. Diamond and Philip H. Dybvig are jointly awarded the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences.
  • Year: 2018 - Hurricane Michael makes landfall in the Florida Panhandle as a catastrophic Category 5 hurricane. It kills 57 people in the United States, 45 in Florida, and causes an estimated $25.1 billion in damage.
  • Year: 2015 - Twin bomb blasts in the Turkish capital Ankara kill 109 and injure 500+.
  • Year: 2010 - The Netherlands Antilles are dissolved as a country.
  • Year: 2009 - Armenia and Turkey sign the Zurich Protocols, intended to normalize relations. However, they are never ratified by either side.
  • Year: 2007 - Sheikh Muszaphar Shukor becomes the first Malaysian in space on board Soyuz TMA-11.
  • Year: 2002 - Iraq War: The United States Congress approves the Authorization for Use of Military Force Against Iraq Resolution of 2002.
  • Year: 1998 - A Lignes Aériennes Congolaises jetliner is shot down by rebels in Kindu, Democratic Republic of the Congo, killing 41 people.
  • Year: 1997 - Austral Líneas Aéreas Flight 2553 crashes and explodes in Uruguay, killing 74.
  • Year: 1986 - A 5.7 Mw  San Salvador earthquake shakes El Salvador, killing 1,500.
  • Year: 1985 - US Navy aircraft intercept an Egyptian airliner carrying the perpetrators of the Achille Lauro hijacking, and force it to land in Italy.
  • Year: 1980 - The Farabundo Martí National Liberation Front is founded in El Salvador.
  • Year: 1980 - The 7.1 Mw  El Asnam earthquake shakes northern Algeria, killing 2,633 and injuring 8,369.
  • Year: 1979 - The Olkiluoto Nuclear Power Plant began operations in Eurajoki, Satakunta, Finland.
  • Year: 1975 - Papua New Guinea joins the United Nations.
  • Year: 1973 - U.S. Vice President Spiro Agnew resigns after being charged with evasion of federal income tax.
  • Year: 1970 - Canada's October Crisis escalates when Quebec Vice Premier Pierre Laporte is kidnapped by members of the Front de libération du Québec.
  • Year: 1970 - Fiji becomes independent.
  • Year: 1967 - The Outer Space Treaty comes into force.
  • Year: 1964 - The Tokyo Summer Olympics opening ceremony is the first to be relayed live by satellites.
  • Year: 1963 - The Partial Nuclear Test Ban Treaty comes into effect.
  • Year: 1963 - France cedes control of the Bizerte naval base to Tunisia.
  • Year: 1957 - The Windscale fire results in Britain's worst nuclear accident.
  • Year: 1957 - U.S. President Dwight D. Eisenhower apologizes to Ghanaian finance minister Komla Agbeli Gbedemah after he is refused service in a Delaware restaurant.
  • Year: 1954 - The Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Sultanate of Muscat, Neil Innes, sends a signal to the Sultanate's forces, accompanied with oil explorers, to penetrate Fahud, marking the beginning of Jebel Akhdar War between the Imamate of Oman and the Sultanate of Muscat.
  • Year: 1945 - The Double Tenth Agreement is signed by the Communist Party and the Kuomintang about the future of China.
  • Year: 1938 - Abiding by the Munich Agreement, Czechoslovakia completes its withdrawal from the Sudetenland.
  • Year: 1935 - In Greece, a coup d'état ends the Second Hellenic Republic.
  • Year: 1933 - A United Airlines Boeing 247 is destroyed by sabotage, the first such proven case in the history of commercial aviation.
  • Year: 1928 - Chiang Kai-shek becomes Chairman of the Republic of China.
  • Year: 1920 - The Carinthian plebiscite determines that the larger part of the Duchy of Carinthia should remain part of Austria.
  • Year: 1918 - RMS Leinster is torpedoed and sunk by UB-123, killing 564, the worst-ever on the Irish Sea.
  • Year: 1913 - U.S. President Woodrow Wilson triggers the explosion of the Gamboa Dike, completing major construction on the Panama Canal.
  • Year: 1911 - The day after a bomb explodes prematurely, the Wuchang Uprising begins against the Chinese monarchy.
  • Year: 1903 - The Women's Social and Political Union is founded in support of the enfranchisement of British women.
  • Year: 1868 - The Ten Years' War begins against Spanish rule in Cuba.
  • Year: 1846 - Triton, the largest moon of the planet Neptune, is discovered by English astronomer William Lassell.
  • Year: 1845 - In Annapolis, Maryland, the Naval School (later the United States Naval Academy) opens with 50 students.
  • Year: 1814 - War of 1812: The United States Revenue Marine attempts to defend the cutter Eagle from the Royal Navy.
  • Year: 1780 - The Great Hurricane of 1780 kills 20,000-30,000 in the Caribbean.
  • Year: 1760 - In a treaty with the Dutch colonial authorities, the Ndyuka people of Suriname – descended from escaped slaves – gain territorial autonomy.
  • Year: 1631 - Thirty Years' War: An army of the Electorate of Saxony seizes Prague.
  • Year: 1580 - Over 600 Papal troops land in Ireland to support the Second Desmond Rebellion.
  • Year: 1575 - Roman Catholic forces under Henry I, Duke of Guise, defeat the Protestants, capturing Philippe de Mornay among others.
  • Year: 1492 - The crew of Christopher Columbus's ship, the Santa Maria, attempt a mutiny.
  • Year: 1471 - Sten Sture the Elder, the Regent of Sweden, with the help of farmers and miners, repels an attack by King Christian I of Denmark.
  • Year: 732 - Charles Martel's forces defeat an Umayyad army near Tours, France.
  • Year: 680 - The Battle of Karbala marks the Martyrdom of Husayn ibn Ali.
