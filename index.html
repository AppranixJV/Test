Historical Events for 01/16

  • Year: 2020 - The United States Senate ratifies the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement as a replacement for NAFTA.
  • Year: 2020 - The first impeachment of Donald Trump formally moves into its trial phase in the United States Senate.
  • Year: 2018 - Myanmar police open fire on a group of ethnic Rakhine protesters, killing seven and wounding twelve.
  • Year: 2017 - Turkish Airlines Flight 6491 crashes into a residential area near Manas International Airport in Kyrgyzstan, killing 39 people.
  • Year: 2016 - Thirty-three out of 126 freed hostages are injured and 23 killed in terrorist attacks in Ouagadougou, Burkina Faso on a hotel and a nearby restaurant.
  • Year: 2012 - The Mali War begins when Tuareg militias start fighting the Malian government for independence.
  • Year: 2011 - Syrian civil war: The Movement for a Democratic Society (TEV-DEM) is established with the stated goal of re-organizing Syria along the lines of democratic confederalism.
  • Year: 2006 - Ellen Johnson Sirleaf is sworn in as Liberia's new president. She becomes Africa's first female elected head of state.
  • Year: 2003 - The Space Shuttle Columbia takes off for mission STS-107 which would be its final one. Columbia disintegrated 16 days later on re-entry.
  • Year: 2002 - War in Afghanistan: The UN Security Council unanimously establishes an arms embargo and the freezing of assets of Osama bin Laden, al-Qaeda, and the remaining members of the Taliban.
  • Year: 2001 - US President Bill Clinton awards former President Theodore Roosevelt a posthumous Medal of Honor for his service in the Spanish-American War.
  • Year: 2001 - Second Congo War: Congolese President Laurent-Désiré Kabila is assassinated by one of his own bodyguards in Kinshasa.
  • Year: 1995 - An avalanche hits the Icelandic village Súðavík, destroying 25 homes and burying 26 people, 14 of whom died.
  • Year: 1992 - El Salvador officials and rebel leaders sign the Chapultepec Peace Accords in Mexico City, Mexico ending the 12-year Salvadoran Civil War that claimed at least 75,000 lives.
  • Year: 1991 - Coalition Forces go to war with Iraq, beginning the Gulf War.
  • Year: 1983 - Turkish Airlines Flight 158 crashes at Ankara Esenboğa Airport in Ankara, Turkey, killing 47 and injuring 20.
  • Year: 1979 - Iranian Revolution: The last Iranian Shah flees Iran with his family for good and relocates to Egypt.
  • Year: 1969 - Space Race: Soviet spacecraft Soyuz 4 and Soyuz 5 perform the first-ever docking of crewed spacecraft in orbit, the first-ever transfer of crew from one space vehicle to another, and the only time such a transfer was accomplished with a space walk.
  • Year: 1969 - Czech student Jan Palach commits suicide by self-immolation in Prague, Czechoslovakia, in protest against the Soviets' crushing of the Prague Spring the year before.
  • Year: 1959 - Austral Líneas Aéreas Flight 205 crashes into the Atlantic Ocean near Astor Piazzolla International Airport in Mar del Plata, Argentina, killing 51.
  • Year: 1945 - World War II: Adolf Hitler moves into his underground bunker, the so-called Führerbunker.
  • Year: 1942 - Crash of TWA Flight 3, killing all 22 aboard, including film star Carole Lombard.
  • Year: 1942 - The Holocaust: Nazi Germany begins deporting Jews from the Łódź Ghetto to Chełmno extermination camp.
  • Year: 1921 - The Marxist Left in Slovakia and the Transcarpathian Ukraine holds its founding congress in Ľubochňa.
  • Year: 1920 - The League of Nations holds its first council meeting in Paris, France.
  • Year: 1919 - Nebraska becomes the 36th state to approve the Eighteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution. With the necessary three-quarters of the states approving the amendment, Prohibition is constitutionally mandated in the United States one year later.
  • Year: 1909 - Ernest Shackleton's expedition finds the magnetic South Pole.
  • Year: 1900 - The United States Senate accepts the Anglo-German treaty of 1899 in which the United Kingdom renounces its claims to the Samoan islands.
  • Year: 1883 - The Pendleton Civil Service Reform Act, establishing the United States Civil Service, is enacted by Congress.
  • Year: 1878 - Russo-Turkish War (1877-78): Battle of Philippopolis: Captain Aleksandr Burago with a squadron of Russian Imperial army dragoons liberates Plovdiv from Ottoman rule.
  • Year: 1862 - Hartley Colliery disaster: Two hundred and four men and boys killed in a mining disaster, prompting a change in UK law which henceforth required all collieries to have at least two independent means of escape.
  • Year: 1847 - Westward expansion of the United States: John C. Frémont is appointed Governor of the new California Territory.
  • Year: 1809 - Peninsular War: The British defeat the French at the Battle of La Coruña.
  • Year: 1786 - Virginia enacts the Statute for Religious Freedom authored by Thomas Jefferson.
  • Year: 1780 - American Revolutionary War: Battle of Cape St. Vincent.
  • Year: 1757 - Forces of the Maratha Empire defeat a 5,000-strong army of the Durrani Empire in the Battle of Narela.
  • Year: 1707 - The Scottish Parliament ratifies the Act of Union, paving the way for the creation of Great Britain.
  • Year: 1605 - The first edition of El ingenioso hidalgo Don Quijote de la Mancha (Book One of Don Quixote) by Miguel de Cervantes is published in Madrid, Spain.
  • Year: 1572 - Thomas Howard, 4th Duke of Norfolk is tried and found guilty of treason for his part in the Ridolfi plot to restore Catholicism in England.
  • Year: 1556 - Philip II becomes King of Spain.
  • Year: 1547 - Grand Duke Ivan IV of Muscovy becomes the first Tsar of Russia, replacing the 264-year-old Grand Duchy of Moscow with the Tsardom of Russia.
  • Year: 1537 - Bigod's Rebellion, an armed insurrection attempting to resist the English Reformation, begins.
  • Year: 1362 - Saint Marcellus's flood kills at least 25,000 people on the shores of the North Sea.
  • Year: 1120 - Crusades: The Council of Nablus is held, establishing the earliest surviving written laws of the Crusader Kingdom of Jerusalem.
  • Year: 929 - Emir Abd-ar-Rahman III establishes the Caliphate of Córdoba.
  • Year: 550 - Gothic War: The Ostrogoths, under King Totila, conquer Rome after a long siege, by bribing the Isaurian garrison.
  • Year: 378 - General Siyaj K'ak' conquers Tikal, enlarging the domain of King Spearthrower Owl of Teotihuacán.
  • Year: 27 BC - Gaius Julius Caesar Octavianus is granted the title Augustus by the Roman Senate, marking the beginning of the Roman Empire.
  • Year: 1458 BC - Hatshepsut dies at the age of 50 and was buried in the Valley of the Kings.
