Historical Events for 04/03

  • Year: 2018 - YouTube headquarters shooting: A 38-year-old gunwoman opens fire at YouTube Headquarters in San Bruno, California, injuring three people before committing suicide.
  • Year: 2017 - A bomb explodes in the St Petersburg metro system, killing 14 and injuring several more people.
  • Year: 2016 - The Panama Papers, a leak of legal documents, reveals information on 214,488 offshore companies.
  • Year: 2013 - More than 50 people die in floods resulting from record-breaking rainfall in La Plata and Buenos Aires, Argentina.
  • Year: 2010 - Apple Inc. released the first generation iPad, a tablet computer.
  • Year: 2009 - Jiverly Antares Wong opens fire at the American Civic Association immigration center in Binghamton, New York, killing thirteen and wounding four before committing suicide.
  • Year: 2008 - Texas law enforcement cordons off the FLDS's YFZ Ranch. Eventually 533 women and children will be taken into state custody.
  • Year: 2008 - ATA Airlines, once one of the ten largest U.S. passenger airlines and largest charter airline, files for bankruptcy for the second time in five years and ceases all operations.
  • Year: 2007 - Conventional-Train World Speed Record: A French TGV train on the LGV Est high speed line sets an official new world speed record.
  • Year: 2004 - Islamic terrorists involved in the 2004 Madrid train bombings are trapped by the police in their apartment and kill themselves.
  • Year: 2000 - United States v. Microsoft Corp.: Microsoft is ruled to have violated United States antitrust law by keeping "an oppressive thumb" on its competitors.
  • Year: 1997 - The Thalit massacre begins in Algeria; all but one of the 53 inhabitants of Thalit are killed by guerrillas.
  • Year: 1996 - A United States Air Force Boeing T-43 crashes near Dubrovnik Airport in Croatia, killing 35, including Secretary of Commerce Ron Brown.
  • Year: 1996 - Suspected "Unabomber" Theodore Kaczynski is captured at his Montana cabin in the United States.
  • Year: 1993 - The outcome of the Grand National horse race is declared void for the first (and only) time
  • Year: 1989 - The US Supreme Court upholds the jurisdictional rights of tribal courts under the Indian Child Welfare Act of 1978 in Mississippi Choctaw Band v. Holyfield.
  • Year: 1981 - The Osborne 1, the first successful portable computer, is unveiled at the West Coast Computer Faire in San Francisco.
  • Year: 1980 - US Congress restores a federal trust relationship with the 501 members of the Shivwits, Kanosh, Koosharem, and the Indian Peaks and Cedar City bands of the Paiute people of Utah.
  • Year: 1975 - Bobby Fischer refuses to play in a chess match against Anatoly Karpov, giving Karpov the title of World Champion by default.
  • Year: 1975 - Vietnam War: Operation Babylift, a mass evacuation of children in the closing stages of the war begins.
  • Year: 1974 - The 1974 Super Outbreak occurs, the second largest tornado outbreak in recorded history (after the 2011 Super Outbreak). The death toll is 315, with nearly 5,500 injured.
  • Year: 1973 - Martin Cooper of Motorola makes the first handheld mobile phone call to Joel S. Engel of Bell Labs.
  • Year: 1969 - Vietnam War: United States Secretary of Defense Melvin Laird announces that the United States will start to "Vietnamize" the war effort.
  • Year: 1968 - Martin Luther King Jr. delivers his "I've Been to the Mountaintop" speech; he was assassinated the next day.
  • Year: 1956 - Hudsonville-Standale tornado: The western half of the Lower Peninsula of Michigan is struck by a deadly F5 tornado.
  • Year: 1955 - The American Civil Liberties Union announces it will defend Allen Ginsberg's book Howl against obscenity charges.
  • Year: 1948 - In Jeju Province, South Korea, a civil-war-like period of violence and human rights abuses known as the Jeju uprising begins.
  • Year: 1948 - Cold War: U.S. President Harry S. Truman signs the Marshall Plan, authorizing $5 billion in aid for 16 countries.
  • Year: 1946 - Japanese Lt. General Masaharu Homma is executed in the Philippines for leading the Bataan Death March.
  • Year: 1942 - World War II: Japanese forces begin an assault on the United States and Filipino troops on the Bataan Peninsula.
  • Year: 1936 - Bruno Richard Hauptmann is executed for the kidnapping and death of Charles Augustus Lindbergh, Jr., the infant son of pilot Charles Lindbergh.
  • Year: 1933 - First flight over Mount Everest, the British Houston-Mount Everest Flight Expedition, led by the Marquis of Clydesdale and funded by Lucy, Lady Houston.
  • Year: 1922 - Joseph Stalin becomes the first General Secretary of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union.
  • Year: 1920 - Attempts are made to carry out the failed assassination attempt on General Mannerheim, led by Aleksander Weckman by order of Eino Rahja, during the White Guard parade in Tampere, Finland.
  • Year: 1895 - The trial in the libel case brought by Oscar Wilde begins, eventually resulting in his imprisonment on charges of homosexuality.
  • Year: 1888 - Jack the Ripper: The first of 11 unsolved brutal murders of women committed in or near the impoverished Whitechapel district in the East End of London, occurs.
  • Year: 1885 - Gottlieb Daimler is granted a German patent for a light, high-speed, four-stroke engine, which he uses seven months later to create the world's first motorcycle, the Daimler Reitwagen.
  • Year: 1882 - American Old West: Robert Ford kills Jesse James.
  • Year: 1865 - American Civil War: Union forces capture Richmond, Virginia, the capital of the Confederate States of America.
  • Year: 1860 - The first successful United States Pony Express run from St. Joseph, Missouri, to Sacramento, California, begins.
  • Year: 1851 - Rama IV is crowned King of Thailand after the death of his half-brother, Rama III.
  • Year: 1721 - Robert Walpole becomes, in effect, the first Prime Minister of Great Britain, though he himself denied that title.
  • Year: 1559 - The second of two treaties making up the Peace of Cateau-Cambrésis is signed, ending the Italian Wars.
  • Year: 1077 - The Patriarchate of Friûl, the first Friulian state, is created.
  • Year: 1043 - Edward the Confessor is crowned King of England.
  • Year: 686 - Maya king Yuknoom Yich'aak K'ahk' assumes the crown of Calakmul.
