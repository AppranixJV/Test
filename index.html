Historical Events for 10/15

  • Year: 2018 - 13-year-old American girl, Jayme Closs, is kidnapped from her Barron, Wisconsin home after her parents were both murdered.
  • Year: 2016 - One hundred and ninety-seven nations amend the Montreal Protocol to include a phase-out of hydrofluorocarbons.
  • Year: 2013 - The 7.2 Mw Bohol earthquake strikes the Philippines. At least 215 were killed.
  • Year: 2008 - The Dow Jones Industrial Average closes down 733.08 points, or 7.87%, the second worst percentage drop in the Dow's history.
  • Year: 2007 - Seventeen activists in New Zealand are arrested in the country's first post-9/11 anti-terrorism raids.
  • Year: 2006 - The 6.7 .mw-parser-output .tooltip-dotted{border-bottom:1px dotted;cursor:help}Mw Kiholo Bay earthquake rocks Hawaii, causing property damage, injuries, landslides, power outages, and the closure of Honolulu International Airport.
  • Year: 2003 - China launches Shenzhou 5, its first crewed space mission.
  • Year: 2001 - NASA's Galileo spacecraft passes within 180 km of Jupiter's moon Io.
  • Year: 1997 - The Cassini probe launches from Cape Canaveral on its way to Saturn.
  • Year: 1994 - The United States, under the Clinton administration, returns Haiti's first democratically elected president, Jean-Bertrand Aristide, to the island.
  • Year: 1991 - The leaders of the Baltic States, Arnold Rüütel of Estonia, Anatolijs Gorbunovs of Latvia and Vytautas Landsbergis of Lithuania, signed the OSCE Final Act in Helsinki, Finland.
  • Year: 1991 - The "Oh-My-God particle", an ultra-high-energy cosmic ray measured at 40,000,000 times that of the highest energy protons produced in a particle accelerator, is observed at the University of Utah HiRes observatory in Dugway Proving Ground, Utah.
  • Year: 1990 - Soviet Union leader Mikhail Gorbachev is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his efforts to lessen Cold War tensions and open up his nation.
  • Year: 1989 - Wayne Gretzky becomes the all-time leading points scorer in the NHL.
  • Year: 1987 - A coup d'état in Burkina Faso overthrows and kills then President Thomas Sankara.
  • Year: 1987 - Aero Trasporti Italiani Flight 460 crashes near Conca di Crezzo, Italy, killing all 37 people on board.
  • Year: 1979 - A coup d'état in El Salvador overthrows President Carlos Humberto Romero and begins the 12 year-long Salvadoran Civil War.
  • Year: 1979 - Supporters of the Malta Labour Party ransack and destroy the Times of Malta building and other locations associated with the Nationalist Party.
  • Year: 1970 - During the construction of Australia's West Gate Bridge, a span of the bridge falls and kills 35 workers. The incident is the country's worst industrial accident to this day.
  • Year: 1966 - The Black Panther Party is created by Huey P. Newton and Bobby Seale.
  • Year: 1965 - Vietnam War: A draft card is burned during an anti-war rally by the Catholic Worker Movement, resulting in the first arrest under a new law.
  • Year: 1956 - FORTRAN, the first modern computer language, is first shared with the coding community.
  • Year: 1954 - Hurricane Hazel devastates the eastern seaboard of North America, killing 95 and causing massive floods as far north as Toronto.
  • Year: 1951 - Mexican chemist Luis E. Miramontes completes the synthesis of norethisterone, the basis of an early oral contraceptive.
  • Year: 1944 - World War II: Germany replaces the Hungarian government after Hungary announces an armistice with the Soviet Union.
  • Year: 1940 - President Lluís Companys of Catalonia is executed by the Francoist government.
  • Year: 1939 - The New York Municipal Airport (later renamed LaGuardia Airport) is dedicated.
  • Year: 1932 - Tata Airlines (later to become Air India) makes its first flight.
  • Year: 1928 - The airship Graf Zeppelin completes its first trans-Atlantic flight, landing at Lakehurst, New Jersey, United States.
  • Year: 1923 - The German Rentenmark is introduced in Germany to counter hyperinflation in the Weimar Republic.
  • Year: 1910 - Airship America is launched from New Jersey in the first attempt to cross the Atlantic by a powered aircraft.
  • Year: 1888 - The "From Hell" letter allegedly sent by Jack the Ripper is received by investigators.
  • Year: 1878 - The Edison Electric Light Company begins operation.
  • Year: 1864 - American Civil War: The Union garrison of Glasgow, Missouri surrenders to Confederate forces.
  • Year: 1863 - American Civil War: The H. L. Hunley, the first submarine to sink a ship, sinks, killing its inventor.
  • Year: 1815 - Napoleon begins his exile on Saint Helena in the South Atlantic Ocean.
  • Year: 1793 - Queen Marie Antoinette of France is tried and convicted of treason.
  • Year: 1783 - The Montgolfier brothers' hot air balloon makes the first human ascent, piloted by Jean-François Pilâtre de Rozier.
  • Year: 1582 - Adoption of the Gregorian calendar begins, eventually leading to near-universal adoption.
  • Year: 1529 - The Siege of Vienna ends when Austria routs the invading Ottoman forces, ending its European expansion.
  • Year: 1211 - Battle of the Rhyndacus: The Latin emperor Henry of Flanders defeats the Nicaean emperor Theodore I Laskaris.
  • Year: 1066 - Following the death of Harold II at the Battle of Hastings, Edgar the Ætheling is proclaimed King of England by the Witan; he is never crowned, and concedes power to William the Conqueror two months later.
