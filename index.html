Historical Events for 03/26

  • Year: 2017 - Russia-wide anti-corruption protests in 99 cities. The Levada Center survey showed that 38% of surveyed Russians supported protests and that 67 percent held Putin personally responsible for high-level corruption.
  • Year: 2010 - The South Korean Navy corvette Cheonan is torpedoed, killing 46 sailors. After an international investigation, the President of the United Nations Security Council blames North Korea.
  • Year: 2005 - Around 200,000 to 300,000 Taiwanese demonstrate in Taipei in opposition to the Anti-Secession Law of China.
  • Year: 1998 - During the Algerian Civil War, the Oued Bouaicha massacre sees fifty-two people, mostly infants, killed with axes and knives.
  • Year: 1997 - Thirty-nine bodies are found in the Heaven's Gate mass suicides.
  • Year: 1991 - Argentina, Brazil, Uruguay and Paraguay sign the Treaty of Asunción, establishing Mercosur, the South Common Market.
  • Year: 1982 - A groundbreaking ceremony for the Vietnam Veterans Memorial is held in Washington, D.C.
  • Year: 1981 - Social Democratic Party (UK) is founded as a party.
  • Year: 1979 - An Interflug Ilyushin Il-18 crashes at Quatro de Fevereiro Airport during a rejected takeoff, killing 10.
  • Year: 1979 - Anwar al-Sadat, Menachem Begin and Jimmy Carter sign the Egypt-Israel peace treaty in Washington, D.C.
  • Year: 1975 - The Biological Weapons Convention comes into force.
  • Year: 1971 - East Pakistan declares its independence from Pakistan to form Bangladesh and the Bangladesh Liberation War begins.
  • Year: 1970 - South Vietnamese President Nguyễn Văn Thiệu implements a land reform program to solve the problem of land tenancy.
  • Year: 1967 - Ten thousand people gather for one of many Central Park be-ins in New York City.
  • Year: 1958 - The African Regroupment Party is launched at a meeting in Paris.
  • Year: 1958 - The United States Army launches Explorer 3.
  • Year: 1955 - Pan Am Flight 845/26 ditches in the Pacific Ocean off the coast of Oregon, killing four.
  • Year: 1954 - Nuclear weapons testing: The Romeo shot of Operation Castle is detonated at Bikini Atoll. Yield: 11 megatons.
  • Year: 1945 - World War II: The Battle of Iwo Jima ends as the island is officially secured by American forces.
  • Year: 1942 - World War II: The first female prisoners arrive at Auschwitz concentration camp in German-occupied Poland.
  • Year: 1939 - Spanish Civil War: Nationalists begin their final offensive of the war.
  • Year: 1934 - The United Kingdom driving test is introduced.
  • Year: 1931 - Ho Chi Minh Communist Youth Union is founded in Vietnam.
  • Year: 1931 - Swissair is founded as the national airline of Switzerland.
  • Year: 1922 - The German Social Democratic Party is founded in Poland.
  • Year: 1917 - World War I: First Battle of Gaza: British troops are halted after 17,000 Turks block their advance.
  • Year: 1915 - The Vancouver Millionaires win the 1915 Stanley Cup Finals, the first championship played between the Pacific Coast Hockey Association and the National Hockey Association.
  • Year: 1913 - First Balkan War: Bulgarian forces capture Adrianople.
  • Year: 1896 - An explosion at the Brunner Mine near Greymouth, New Zealand kills 65 coal miners in the country's worst industrial accident.
  • Year: 1885 - The Métis people of the District of Saskatchewan under Louis Riel begin the North-West Rebellion against Canada.
  • Year: 1871 - The elections of Commune council of the Paris Commune are held.
  • Year: 1839 - The first Henley Royal Regatta is held.
  • Year: 1830 - The Book of Mormon is published in Palmyra, New York.
  • Year: 1812 - A political cartoon in the Boston-Gazette coins the term "gerrymander" to describe oddly shaped electoral districts designed to help incumbents win reelection.
  • Year: 1812 - An earthquake devastates Caracas, Venezuela.
  • Year: 1700 - William Dampier is the first European to circumnavigate New Britain, discovering it is an island (which he names Nova Britannia) rather than part of New Guinea.
  • Year: 1697 - Safavid government troops take control of Basra.
  • Year: 1651 - Silver-loaded Spanish ship San José is pushed south by strong winds, subsequently it wrecks in the coast of southern Chile and its surviving crew is killed by indigenous Cuncos.
  • Year: 1640 - The Royal Academy of Turku, the first university of Finland, is founded in the city of Turku by Queen Christina of Sweden at the proposal of Count Per Brahe.
  • Year: 1636 - Utrecht University is founded in the Netherlands.
  • Year: 1552 - Guru Amar Das becomes the Third Sikh guru.
  • Year: 1484 - William Caxton prints his translation of Aesop's Fables.
  • Year: 1351 - Combat of the Thirty: Thirty Breton knights call out and defeat thirty English knights.
  • Year: 1344 - The Siege of Algeciras, one of the first European military engagements where gunpowder was used, comes to an end.
  • Year: 1169 - Saladin becomes the emir of Egypt.
  • Year: 1027 - Pope John XIX crowns Conrad II as Holy Roman Emperor.
  • Year: 1021 - The death of the Fatimid caliph al-Hakim bi-Amr Allah, kept secret for six weeks, is announced, along with the succession of his son, al-Zahir li-i'zaz Din Allah.
  • Year: 590 - Emperor Maurice proclaims his son Theodosius as co-emperor of the Byzantine Empire.
