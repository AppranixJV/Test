Historical Events for 12/30

  • Year: 2013 - More than 100 people are killed when anti-government forces attack key buildings in Kinshasa, Democratic Republic of the Congo.
  • Year: 2009 - A suicide bomber kills nine people at Forward Operating Base Chapman, a key facility of the Central Intelligence Agency in Afghanistan.
  • Year: 2009 - A segment of the Lanzhou-Zhengzhou-Changsha pipeline ruptures in Shaanxi, China, and approximately 150,000 L (40,000 US gal) of diesel oil flows down the Wei River before finally reaching the Yellow River.
  • Year: 2006 - Former President of Iraq Saddam Hussein is executed.
  • Year: 2006 - The Indonesian passenger ferry MV Senopati Nusantara sinks in a storm, resulting in at least 400 deaths.
  • Year: 2006 - Madrid-Barajas Airport is bombed.
  • Year: 2005 - Tropical Storm Zeta forms in the open Atlantic Ocean, tying the record for the latest tropical cyclone ever to form in the North Atlantic basin.
  • Year: 2004 - A fire in the República Cromagnon nightclub in Buenos Aires, Argentina, kills 194.
  • Year: 2000 - Rizal Day bombings: A series of bombs explode in various places in Metro Manila, Philippines within a period of a few hours, killing 22 and injuring about a hundred.
  • Year: 1997 - In the worst incident in Algeria's insurgency, the Wilaya of Relizane massacres, 400 people from four villages are killed.
  • Year: 1996 - Proposed budget cuts by Benjamin Netanyahu spark protests from 250,000 workers who shut down services across Israel.
  • Year: 1993 - Israel establishes diplomatic relations with Vatican City and also upgrades to full diplomatic relations with Ireland.
  • Year: 1987 - Stella Sigcau, Prime minister of the South African Bantustan of Transkei, is ousted from power in a bloodless military coup led by General Bantu Holomisa.
  • Year: 1972 - Vietnam War: Operation Linebacker II ends.
  • Year: 1967 - Aeroflot Flight L-51 crashes near Liepāja International Airport in Liepāja, Latvia, killing 43.
  • Year: 1958 - The Guatemalan Air Force sinks several Mexican fishing boats alleged to have breached maritime borders, killing three and sparking international tension.
  • Year: 1954 - The Finnish National Bureau of Investigation is established to consolidate criminal investigation and intelligence into a single agency.
  • Year: 1952 - An RAF Avro Lancaster bomber crashes in Luqa, Malta after an engine failure, killing three crew members and a civilian on the ground.
  • Year: 1947 - Cold War: King Michael I of Romania is forced to abdicate by the Soviet Union-backed Communist government of Romania.
  • Year: 1944 - King George II of Greece declares a regency, leaving the throne vacant.
  • Year: 1943 - Subhas Chandra Bose raises the flag of Indian independence at Port Blair.
  • Year: 1936 - The Flint sit-down strike hits General Motors.
  • Year: 1935 - The Italian Air Force bombs a Swedish Red Cross hospital during the Second Italo-Abyssinian War.
  • Year: 1927 - The Ginza Line, the first subway line in Asia, opens in Tokyo, Japan.
  • Year: 1922 - The Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR) is formed.
  • Year: 1916 - The last coronation in Hungary is performed for King Charles IV and Queen Zita.
  • Year: 1916 - Russian mystic and advisor to the Tsar Grigori Yefimovich Rasputin is murdered by a loyalist group led by Prince Felix Yusupov. His frozen, partially-trussed body was discovered in a Petrograd river three days later.
  • Year: 1906 - The All-India Muslim League is founded in Dacca, East Bengal, British India (later Dhaka, Bangladesh).
  • Year: 1905 - Former Idaho Governor Frank Steunenberg is assassinated at the front gate of his home in Caldwell.
  • Year: 1903 - A fire at the Iroquois Theater in Chicago, Illinois kills at least 605.
  • Year: 1902 - The Discovery Expedition under Robert Falcon Scott attained a Farthest South at 82°17′S in Antarctica.
  • Year: 1897 - The British Colony of Natal annexes Zululand.
  • Year: 1896 - Canadian ice hockey player Ernie McLea scores the first hat-trick in Stanley Cup play, and the Cup-winning goal as the Montreal Victorias defeat the Winnipeg Victorias 6-5.
  • Year: 1896 - Filipino patriot and reform advocate José Rizal is executed by a Spanish firing squad in Manila.
  • Year: 1890 - Following the Wounded Knee Massacre, the United States Army and Lakota warriors face off in the Drexel Mission Fight.
  • Year: 1853 - Gadsden Purchase: The United States buys land from Mexico to facilitate railroad building in the Southwest.
  • Year: 1825 - The Treaty of St. Louis between the United States and the Shawnee Nation is proclaimed.
  • Year: 1816 - The Treaty of St. Louis between the United States and the united Ottawa, Ojibwa, and Potawatomi Indian tribes is proclaimed.
  • Year: 1813 - War of 1812: British soldiers burn Buffalo, New York.
  • Year: 1702 - Queen Anne's War: James Moore, Governor of the Province of Carolina, abandons the Siege of St. Augustine.
  • Year: 1460 - Wars of the Roses: Lancastrians kill the 3rd Duke of York and win the Battle of Wakefield.
  • Year: 1419 - Hundred Years' War: Battle of La Rochelle.
  • Year: 1066 - Granada massacre: A Muslim mob storms the royal palace in Granada, crucifies Jewish vizier Joseph ibn Naghrela and massacres most of the Jewish population of the city.
  • Year: 999 - Battle of Glenmama: The combined forces of Munster and Meath under king Brian Boru inflict a crushing defeat on the allied armies of Leinster and Dublin near Lyons Hill in Ireland.
  • Year: 534 - The second and final edition of the Code of Justinian comes into effect in the Byzantine Empire.
