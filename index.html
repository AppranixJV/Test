Historical Events for 02/25

  • Year: 1999 - Alitalia Flight 1553 crashes at Genoa Cristoforo Colombo Airport in Genoa, Italy, killing four.
  • Year: 1994 - American-Israeli extremist Baruch Goldstein commits a mass shooting at the Cave of the Patriarchs mausoleum, leaving 29 dead and over 100 injured before he was disarmed and beaten to death by survivors.
  • Year: 1991 - Disbandment of the Warsaw Pact at a meeting of its members in Budapest.
  • Year: 1986 - People Power Revolution: President of the Philippines Ferdinand Marcos flees the nation after 20 years of rule; Corazon Aquino becomes the Philippines' first female president.
  • Year: 1980 - The government of Suriname is overthrown by a military coup led by Dési Bouterse.
  • Year: 1956 - In his speech On the Cult of Personality and Its Consequences, Nikita Khrushchev, leader of the Soviet Union, denounces Stalin.
  • Year: 1951 - The first Pan American Games are officially opened in Buenos Aires by Argentine President Juan Perón.
  • Year: 1948 - In a coup d'état led by Klement Gottwald, the Communist Party of Czechoslovakia takes control of government in Prague to end the Third Czechoslovak Republic.
  • Year: 1947 - Soviet NKVD forces in Hungary abduct Béla Kovács—secretary-general of the majority Independent Smallholders' Party—and deport him to the USSR in defiance of Parliament. His arrest is an important turning point in the Communist takeover of Hungary.
  • Year: 1947 - The formal abolition of Prussia is proclaimed by the Allied Control Council, the Prussian government having already been abolished by the Preußenschlag of 1932.
  • Year: 1941 - The outlawed Communist Party of the Netherlands organises a general strike in German-occupied Amsterdam to protest against Nazi persecution of Dutch Jews.
  • Year: 1939 - As part of British air raid precautions, the first of 2.5 million Anderson shelters is constructed in a garden in Islington, north London.
  • Year: 1933 - Launch of the USS Ranger at Newport News, Virginia. It is the first purpose-built aircraft carrier to be commissioned by the US Navy.
  • Year: 1932 - Hitler, having been stateless for seven years, obtains German citizenship when he is appointed a Brunswick state official by Dietrich Klagges, a fellow Nazi. As a result, Hitler is able to run for Reichspräsident in the 1932 election.
  • Year: 1921 - Georgian capital Tbilisi falls to the invading Russian forces after heavy fighting and the Russians declare the Georgian Soviet Socialist Republic.
  • Year: 1918 - World War I: German forces capture Tallinn to virtually complete the occupation of Estonia.
  • Year: 1916 - World War I: In the Battle of Verdun, a German unit captures Fort Douaumont, keystone of the French defences, without a fight.
  • Year: 1912 - Marie-Adélaïde, the eldest of six daughters of Guillaume IV, becomes the first reigning Grand Duchess of Luxembourg.
  • Year: 1875 - Guangxu Emperor of Qing dynasty China begins his reign, under Empress Dowager Cixi's regency.
  • Year: 1870 - Hiram Rhodes Revels, a Republican from Mississippi, is sworn into the United States Senate, becoming the first African American ever to sit in Congress.
  • Year: 1843 - Lord George Paulet occupies the Kingdom of Hawaii in the name of Great Britain in the Paulet affair.
  • Year: 1836 - Samuel Colt is granted a United States patent for his revolver firearm.
  • Year: 1705 - George Frideric Handel's opera Nero premiered in Hamburg.
  • Year: 1336 - Four thousand defenders of Pilenai commit mass suicide rather than be taken captive by the Teutonic Knights.
  • Year: 628 - Khosrow II, the last great Shah of the Sasanian Empire (Iran), is overthrown by his son Kavadh II.
  • Year: 138 - Roman emperor Hadrian adopts Antoninus Pius as his son, effectively making him his successor.
