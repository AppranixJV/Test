Historical Events for 10/13

  • Year: 2019 - Kenyan Brigid Kosgei sets a new world record for a woman runner with a time of 2:14:04 at the 2019 Chicago Marathon.
  • Year: 2016 - The Maldives announces its decision to withdraw from the Commonwealth of Nations.
  • Year: 2013 - A stampede occurs in India during the Hindu festival Navratri, killing 115 and injuring more than 110.
  • Year: 2010 - The mining accident in Copiapó, Chile ends as all 33 trapped miners arrive at the surface after a record 69 days underground.
  • Year: 1993 - At least 60 people die in eastern Papua New Guinea when a series of earthquakes rock the Finisterre Range, triggering massive landslides.
  • Year: 1990 - Syrian forces attack free areas of Lebanon, removing General Michel Aoun from the presidential palace.
  • Year: 1983 - Ameritech Mobile Communications launches the first US cellular network in Chicago.
  • Year: 1977 - Hijacking of Lufthansa Flight 181 by the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine.
  • Year: 1976 - The first electron micrograph of an Ebola virus is taken at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention by Dr. F. A. Murphy.
  • Year: 1972 - Uruguayan Air Force Flight 571 crashes in the Andes mountains. Twenty-eight survive the crash. All but 16 succumb before rescue on December 23.
  • Year: 1972 - Aeroflot Flight 217 crashes outside Moscow, killing 174.
  • Year: 1962 - The Pacific Northwest experiences a cyclone the equal of a Category 3 hurricane, with winds above 150 mph. Forty-six people die.
  • Year: 1946 - France adopts the constitution of the Fourth Republic.
  • Year: 1944 - World War II: The Soviet Riga Offensive captures the city.
  • Year: 1943 - World War II: Marshal Pietro Badoglio announces that Italy has officially declared war on Germany.
  • Year: 1923 - Ankara becomes the capital of Turkey.
  • Year: 1921 - Soviet republics sign the Treaty of Kars to formalize the borders between Turkey and the South Caucasus states.
  • Year: 1917 - The "Miracle of the Sun" is witnessed by an estimated 70,000 people in the Cova da Iria in Portugal.
  • Year: 1915 - First World War: The Battle of the Hohenzollern Redoubt marks the end of the Battle of Loos.
  • Year: 1911 - Prince Arthur, Duke of Connaught and Strathearn, becomes the first Governor General of Canada of royal descent.
  • Year: 1908 - Margaret Travers Symons bursts into the UK parliament and becomes the first woman to speak there.
  • Year: 1903 - The Boston Red Sox win the first modern World Series, defeating the Pittsburgh Pirates in the eighth game.
  • Year: 1892 - Edward Emerson Barnard is first to discover a comet by photographic means.
  • Year: 1885 - The Georgia Institute of Technology is founded in Atlanta, Georgia.
  • Year: 1881 - First known conversation in modern Hebrew by Eliezer Ben-Yehuda and friends.
  • Year: 1843 - In New York City, B'nai B'rith, the oldest Jewish service organization in the world, is founded.
  • Year: 1821 - The Declaration of Independence of the Mexican Empire is publicly proclaimed.
  • Year: 1812 - War of 1812: Sir Isaac Brock's British and native forces repel an invasion of Canada by General Rensselaer's United States forces.
  • Year: 1793 - French Revolutionary Wars: Austro-Prussian victory over Republican France at the First Battle of Wissembourg.
  • Year: 1792 - In Washington, D.C., the cornerstone of the United States Executive Mansion (known as the White House since 1818) is laid.
  • Year: 1775 - The Continental Congress establishes the Continental Navy (predecessor of the United States Navy).
  • Year: 1710 - Port Royal, the capital of French Acadia, falls in a siege by British forces.
  • Year: 1644 - A Swedish-Dutch fleet defeats the Danish fleet at Fehmarn and captures about 1,000 prisoners.
  • Year: 1399 - Coronation of Henry IV of England at Westminster Abbey.
  • Year: 1332 - Rinchinbal Khan becomes the Khagan of the Mongols and Emperor of the Yuan dynasty, reigning for only 53 days.
  • Year: 1307 - Hundreds of the Knights Templar in France are arrested at dawn by King Philip the Fair, and later confess under torture to heresy.
  • Year: 1269 - The present church building at Westminster Abbey is consecrated.
  • Year: 409 - Vandals and Alans cross the Pyrenees and appear in Hispania.
  • Year: 54 - Roman emperor Claudius dies from poisoning under mysterious circumstances. He is succeeded by his adoptive son Nero, rather than by Britannicus, his son with Messalina.
